## Slide 3: Making Polite Requests 🙏
### Asking Someone to Do Something for You

A **request** is when you ask someone to do something.  
The more formal or indirect the structure, the more polite it sounds.

**Structure overview:**

| Structure | Formality | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Can you...?** | informal | *Can you pass the salt?* |
| **Could you...?** | neutral / polite | *Could you send me the file?* |
| **Would you...?** | polite | *Would you help me with this?* |
| **Would you mind...?** | polite / careful | *Would you mind waiting a moment?* |
| **Could you possibly...?** | very polite | *Could you possibly reschedule the meeting?* |
| **I was wondering if you could...** | formal | *I was wondering if you could review my report.* |

> 💡 **Tip:** After "Would you mind...?" use the **-ing** form of the verb.  
> ✅ *Would you mind **closing** the door?*  
> ❌ *Would you mind close the door?*

## Slide 4: Responding to Requests 💬
### Saying Yes or No Politely

Knowing how to accept or refuse a request is just as important as making one.

**Accepting a request:**

| Response | Register |
|---|---|
| *Sure!* / *Of course!* | informal |
| *No problem at all.* | neutral |
| *Certainly. I'll do it right away.* | formal |
| *I'd be happy to help.* | polite / warm |
| *Sure, leave it to me.* | confident, informal |

**Declining a request:**

| Response | Register |
|---|---|
| *Sorry, I can't right now.* | informal |
| *I'm afraid I won't be able to.* | polite |
| *Unfortunately, that's not possible for me today.* | formal |
| *I wish I could, but I have another commitment.* | polite / apologetic |

> 💡 When declining, always offer a brief reason or apology — it sounds much more natural and respectful.

## Slide 5: Making Offers 🤝
### Volunteering to Do Something for Someone

An **offer** is when you volunteer to do something to help someone else.

**Common structures:**

| Structure | Example | Translation |
|---|---|---|
| **Shall I...?** | *Shall I carry that for you?* | Quer que eu carregue isso? |
| **I'll...** | *I'll get the door.* | Eu abro a porta. |
| **Would you like me to...?** | *Would you like me to explain again?* | Quer que eu explique de novo? |
| **Can I...?** | *Can I help you with that?* | Posso te ajudar com isso? |
| **Let me...** | *Let me check that for you.* | Deixa eu verificar isso pra você. |
| **Do you want me to...?** | *Do you want me to call a taxi?* | Quer que eu chame um táxi? |

> 💡 **"Shall I...?"** is typically British English and sounds slightly more formal.  
> **"I'll..."** is the most natural and confident way to offer in everyday speech.

---

## Slide 6: Responding to Offers 🌟
### Accepting or Politely Refusing Help

**Accepting an offer:**

| Response | Register |
|---|---|
| *Yes, please! That would be great.* | warm, common |
| *That's very kind of you. Thank you.* | polite / formal |
| *Please do! I'd appreciate it.* | enthusiastic |
| *Thanks, that would really help.* | casual / friendly |

**Declining an offer:**

| Response | Register |
|---|---|
| *No, thanks. I'm fine.* | casual |
| *That's very kind, but I can manage.* | polite |
| *Thank you, but it's all right — I've got it.* | polite / independent |
| *I appreciate it, but I don't need any help right now.* | formal |

> 💡 Always acknowledge the other person's intention — say "thank you" or "that's kind" even when you refuse.

## Slide 7: Asking for Permission 🔑
### Requesting to Do Something Yourself

**Permission** is when you ask if *you* are allowed to do something.

| Structure | Formality | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Can I...?** | informal | *Can I use your phone?* |
| **Could I...?** | neutral / polite | *Could I leave a bit early today?* |
| **May I...?** | formal | *May I speak with the manager, please?* |
| **Is it OK if I...?** | neutral / casual | *Is it OK if I bring a friend?* |
| **Do you mind if I...?** | polite / considerate | *Do you mind if I open the window?* |
| **Would it be all right if I...?** | formal / careful | *Would it be all right if I worked from home tomorrow?* |

> 💡 **"May I...?"** is more formal and traditional — great for professional or academic settings.  
> **"Is it OK if I...?"** is natural and very common in informal situations.

---

## Slide 8: Responding to Permission Requests ✅❌
### Granting or Refusing Permission

**Granting permission:**

| Response | Register |
|---|---|
| *Sure, go ahead.* | casual |
| *Yes, of course!* | friendly |
| *Absolutely. No problem at all.* | enthusiastic |
| *Yes, you're welcome to.* | polite |
| *Feel free.* | informal / open |

**Refusing permission:**

| Response | Register |
|---|---|
| *Sorry, I'd rather you didn't.* | polite but firm |
| *I'm afraid that's not possible.* | formal |
| *Actually, I'd prefer if you didn't.* | soft refusal |
| *Unfortunately, that's not allowed here.* | rule-based |

> 💡 Answering *"Do you mind if I...?"* can be tricky in English:
> - **"No, not at all."** = I **don't** mind → it's OK ✅
> - **"Yes, actually."** = I **do** mind → it's NOT OK ❌

## Slide 9: Formal vs Informal Register 🎩👕
### Choosing the Right Level of Politeness

The same idea can be expressed in very different ways depending on the situation.

**Scenario: You want to borrow someone's pen.**

| Register | Request |
|---|---|
| Very informal | *Hey, lend me your pen.* |
| Neutral | *Can I borrow your pen?* |
| Polite | *Could I borrow your pen for a second?* |
| Very polite | *Would you mind if I borrowed your pen?* |

**Scenario: You want a colleague to review a document.**

| Register | Request |
|---|---|
| Informal | *Can you check this for me?* |
| Polite | *Could you take a look at this?* |
| Formal | *I was wondering if you could review this document at your earliest convenience.* |

> 💡 **Key rule:** The longer and more indirect the request, the more polite it sounds.  
> Short = direct (can sound rude in formal contexts).  
> Long = indirect (sounds careful and respectful).

## Slide 10: Common Mistakes 🚫
### Errors Brazilian Students Often Make

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Why? |
|---|---|---|
| *Would you mind to close the door?* | *Would you mind **closing** the door?* | After "mind" → use **-ing** |
| *Can I to use your pen?* | *Can I use your pen?* | No "to" after modal verbs |
| *May you help me?* | *Could you help me?* | "May" is for permission, not requests |
| *Do you mind if I will open the window?* | *Do you mind if I **open** the window?* | Use simple present after "if" |
| *I am wanting to ask a favor.* | *I want to ask a favor.* | Stative verbs don't use continuous |
| *Yes, I mind.* (meaning "go ahead") | *No, I don't mind.* | Answering "Do you mind" correctly |
| *Shall you help me?* | *Shall I help you?* | "Shall" for offers uses **I** or **we** |

## Slide 11: Putting It All Together 🌱
### Requests, Offers & Permissions in a Real Conversation

**Context:** At the office — a new employee talking to a colleague.

---

> **Employee:** *Excuse me, could I ask you a quick question?*  
> **Colleague:** *Of course! Go ahead.*  
> **Employee:** *Would you mind showing me how to use the printer?*  
> **Colleague:** *Sure, I'd be happy to. Let me show you right now.*  
> **Employee:** *Thank you so much. Oh — is it OK if I print in color?*  
> **Colleague:** *I'm afraid that's not allowed for regular documents. Black and white only.*  
> **Employee:** *No problem. I appreciate your help!*  

---

**Notice how the conversation uses:**
- ✅ A polite request opener: *"Excuse me, could I...?"*
- ✅ A "would you mind" request with **-ing**: *"Would you mind **showing**...?"*
- ✅ A natural offer with **"Let me"**: *"Let me show you..."*
- ✅ A casual permission question: *"Is it OK if I...?"*
- ✅ A formal refusal with reason: *"I'm afraid that's not allowed..."*
