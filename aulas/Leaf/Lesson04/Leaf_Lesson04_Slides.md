## Slide 2: Warm-Up Activity 🎯
### Never Have I Ever
#### Aquecimento: "Nunca Fiz Isso" — Versão Educacional

---

**🎮 How to Play:**

1. The teacher reads a sentence with **"I have never..."**
2. Students who **HAVE done it** raise their hand ✋
3. Those students must say a **complete sentence** using:
   - **Present Perfect** → for the experience: *"I **have** eaten sushi."*
   - **Simple Past** → for the details: *"I **ate** it in São Paulo last year."*

---

**📋 Teacher's Statement List — Read these aloud:**

| # | Statement (Teacher reads) | Expected student response |
|---|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | I have never eaten sushi. | "I have eaten sushi. I ate it in [place] [time]." |
| 2 | I have never traveled outside Brazil. | "I have traveled abroad. I went to [country] in [year]." |
| 3 | I have never watched a movie in English. | "I have watched one! I watched [title] last month." |
| 4 | I have never tried a food I really hated. | "I have tried something terrible! I ate [food] and hated it." |
| 5 | I have never ridden a horse. | "I have ridden a horse! I rode one in [place]." |
| 6 | I have never sent an email in English. | "I have sent one! I sent it to [a teacher / a company]." |
| 7 | I have never stayed awake all night. | "I have stayed up all night! It happened during [event]." |
| 8 | I have never won a competition. | "I have won a competition! I won [type] when I was [age]." |
| 9 | I have never cooked a meal for many people. | "I have done that! I cooked for [number] people at [occasion]." |
| 10 | I have never met a famous person. | "I have met someone famous! I met [name] at [place]." |
| 11 | I have never learned to play a musical instrument. | "I have learned! I played [instrument] for [time]." |
| 12 | I have never seen the ocean. | "I have seen it! I went to the beach in [place] in [year]." |

---

**🎯 Pedagogical Goal:**
This game naturally **activates the contrast between Present Perfect and Simple Past**:
- **Present Perfect** = the *fact* of the experience (no specific time): *"I have eaten sushi"*
- **Simple Past** = the *details* of when/where/how: *"I ate it last Friday in Liberdade"*

Students discover the grammar through real communication before seeing the formal rules.

---

**[Teacher Note: Keep the game fast and fun — about 8–10 minutes. Don't correct every error at this stage. Make mental notes of common mistakes (e.g., "I have ate", "I ate already") to address in Slides 5 and 6. If a student says only the Simple Past ("I ate sushi"), gently prompt: "Great! But first — have you eaten it? Start with 'I have eaten...'". The goal is engagement and activation, not perfection. Small groups of up to 5 students make this especially dynamic — encourage everyone to respond.]**

## Slide 3: Key Vocabulary 📚
### Part 1: Core Adverbs & Expressions

---

| Word / Expression | Translation (PT) | Position in Sentence | Example Sentence |
|-------------------|-----------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **experience** | experiência | subject / object | *"Traveling alone was an amazing **experience**."* |
| **already** | já (com expectativa) | after *have/has* → before main verb | *"I have **already** finished my homework."* |
| **yet** | ainda (negativa) / já (pergunta) | end of sentence | *"I haven't eaten **yet**." / "Have you finished **yet**?"* |
| **never** | nunca | after *have/has* → before main verb | *"She has **never** tried Indian food."* |
| **ever** | alguma vez | after subject → before main verb (questions) | *"Have you **ever** been to London?"* |
| **just** | acabou de (agora mesmo) | after *have/has* → before main verb | *"He has **just** arrived. He's still at the door."* |

---

**📌 Position Rule — Quick Reference:**

```
AFTER have/has (before main verb):
  ✅ I have ALREADY seen this.
  ✅ She has NEVER tried it.
  ✅ He has JUST called.

END of sentence:
  ✅ I haven't finished YET.
  ✅ Have you eaten YET?
```

---

**⚠️ Common Errors — Erros Comuns:**

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| *"I yet haven't finished."* | *"I haven't finished **yet**."* | *yet* goes to the END in negatives |
| *"Have you ever been never there?"* | *"Have you **ever** been there?"* | Use *ever* OR *never*, not both |
| *"I have ate already."* | *"I have **already eaten**."* | *already* comes BEFORE the main verb |

---

**[Teacher Note: Ask students to translate each word and identify the position in their own sentences. You can do a quick drill: write 3 incomplete sentences on the board (or say them aloud) and ask students to place the correct adverb in the right position. For example: "I have _____ finished." (already) / "Have you eaten ______?" (yet) / "She has _____ called." (just). This reinforces position rules before moving to full grammar explanation.]**

## Slide 4: Key Vocabulary 📚
### Part 2: Time Expressions

---

| Word / Expression | Translation (PT) | Typical Position | Example Sentence |
|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **recently** | recentemente | beginning OR end of sentence | *"I have **recently** started going to the gym."* / *"I've joined a new club **recently**."* |
| **so far** | até agora, até o momento | beginning OR end of sentence | *"**So far**, I have studied three chapters."* / *"I've learned 50 words **so far**."* |
| **up to now** | até agora, até este momento | usually at the beginning | *"**Up to now**, I have never missed a class."* |
| **still (haven't)** | ainda não | before *haven't/hasn't* | *"I **still haven't** finished the book."* (stronger than "haven't yet") |

---

**📌 Meaning & Usage — Significado e Uso:**

| Expression | What it means in context | Example in conversation |
|------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| **recently** | In the near past, not long ago — *não faz muito tempo* | *"Have you done anything fun recently?"* → *"Yes! I've recently visited the new museum."* |
| **so far** | From the beginning until this moment — *desde o início até agora* | *"So far, this has been the best trip of my life."* |
| **up to now** | Everything from the past until this moment — *até este instante* | *"Up to now, I haven't had any problems with the course."* |
| **still haven't** | Emphasizes that something expected hasn't happened — *surpreendentemente, ainda não* | *"I still haven't watched that show everyone loves!"* |

---

**🗣️ Natural Conversation Examples — Como soam na conversa:**

> *"What have you been doing **recently**?"*  
> → *"I've **recently** started learning to cook. So far, I've only made pasta."*

> *"How's the new project going?"*  
> → *"**So far, so good!** We haven't had any issues **up to now**."*

> *"Have you read that book I recommended?"*  
> → *"No, I **still haven't** found the time. I've been really busy **recently**."*

---

**[Teacher Note: These expressions are frequently used in natural, flowing English conversation. Point out that *so far* is extremely common in both formal and informal contexts (emails, presentations, chats). "So far, so good" is a very useful idiom worth highlighting. Also note that *still haven't* carries an emotional nuance — it implies the speaker (or listener) expected it to happen by now. Have students practice creating their own sentences with each expression. A quick round of "complete the sentence with your own life" works well here: "So far in this English course, I have...".]**

## Slide 5: Present Perfect — Formation & Uses 📖
### Formação e Usos do Present Perfect

---

### 🔧 Formation — Formação

| Form | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Affirmative** | Subject + **have/has** + past participle | *"I **have seen** that film."* |
| **Negative** | Subject + **haven't/hasn't** + past participle | *"She **hasn't visited** Rome yet."* |
| **Question** | **Have/Has** + subject + past participle? | *"**Have you ever tried** sushi?"* |
| **Short Answer (+)** | Yes, I/you/we/they **have**. / Yes, he/she/it **has**. | *"Yes, I have!"* |
| **Short Answer (-)** | No, I/you/we/they **haven't**. / No, he/she/it **hasn't**. | *"No, she hasn't."* |

---

### ⚡ Contractions — Formas Contraídas

| Full Form | Contracted | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| I have | **I've** | *"**I've** traveled to five countries."* |
| You have | **You've** | *"**You've** never tried this before."* |
| He has | **He's** | *"**He's** just called you."* |
| She has | **She's** | *"**She's** already finished."* |
| We have | **We've** | *"**We've** known each other for years."* |
| They have | **They've** | *"**They've** never seen snow."* |

> ⚠️ Note: *He's* can mean *He is* OR *He has*. Context makes it clear!  
> *"He's a doctor."* (= He is) vs. *"He's worked here for 10 years."* (= He has)

---

### 🎯 Main Uses — Principais Usos

**1. Life Experiences — without a specific time (sem tempo específico)**
> *"I **have visited** Japan."* — We don't know (or it doesn't matter) exactly when.  
> *"She **has never eaten** mango."* — A fact about her life up to now.

**2. Recent Actions with Present Relevance — resultado no presente**
> *"I **have lost** my keys."* — I lost them at some point → I don't have them NOW.  
> *"She **has just arrived**."* — Very recent action → she is HERE now.

**3. With already / yet / just / recently / so far / up to now**
> *"Have you **already** booked the tickets?"*  
> *"I **haven't** packed my bag **yet**."*  
> *"They've **just** finished the meeting."*

---

### ❌ / ✅ Common Brazilian Errors — Erros comuns de brasileiros

| ❌ Common Mistake | ✅ Correct Form | Why? |
|------------------|----------------|------|
| *"I have seen him yesterday."* | *"I **saw** him yesterday."* | *Yesterday* = specific time → Simple Past |
| *"Did you ever try sushi?"* | *"**Have** you ever tried sushi?"* | Life experiences → Present Perfect |
| *"I have already went there."* | *"I have already **gone** there."* | Past participle of *go* = **gone**, not *went* |
| *"She didn't arrived yet."* | *"She **hasn't arrived** yet."* | Negative of PP uses *haven't/hasn't* + participle |
| *"I know him since 2015."* | *"I **have known** him since 2015."* | Duration from past to present → Present Perfect |

---

**[Teacher Note: Emphasize Use #1 (life experiences) because this is the core focus of today's lesson. Use #2 and #3 give context for the adverbs covered in Slides 3 and 4. The contractions table is especially important — Brazilian students often avoid contractions in speaking, which sounds unnatural. Encourage them to practice "I've", "She's", etc. For the error table, have students read the incorrect sentences aloud first and then self-correct. This activates error awareness. The "I have seen him yesterday" error is the most critical one — it will be addressed in more depth in Slide 6.]**

## Slide 6: Present Perfect vs Simple Past ⚖️
### Quando usar cada um — Side-by-Side Comparison

---

### 📊 Comparison Table — Tabela Comparativa

| | **Present Perfect** | **Simple Past** |
|---|---|---|
| **When to use** | Experiences with no specific time; actions connected to the present | Completed actions with a specific time in the past |
| **Time signals** | *ever, never, already, yet, just, recently, so far, up to now* | *yesterday, last week, in 2019, ago, when I was young, at 3pm* |
| **Relationship to now** | Connected to the present moment | Completely finished — no connection to now |
| **Auxiliary** | have / has + past participle | did (negative) / no auxiliary (affirmative) |
| **Question form** | Have you...? / Has she...? | Did you...? / Did she...? |
| **Negative form** | I haven't / She hasn't | I didn't / She didn't |

---

### 🔄 Side-by-Side Examples — Exemplos Comparativos

**Pair 1: Travel Experience**
| Present Perfect | Simple Past |
|----------------|------------|
| *"I **have visited** Paris."* | *"I **visited** Paris **in 2018**."* |
| → We only know you've been there | → We know exactly when |
| → No specific time — just the experience | → Specific time marker: *in 2018* |

**Pair 2: Food Experience**
| Present Perfect | Simple Past |
|----------------|------------|
| *"**Have** you **eaten** Japanese food?"* | *"**Did** you **eat** sushi **yesterday**?"* |
| → Asking about any time in your life | → Asking about a specific occasion |

**Pair 3: Recent vs Specific**
| Present Perfect | Simple Past |
|----------------|------------|
| *"She **has just arrived**."* | *"She **arrived** 10 minutes ago."* |
| → Focus on NOW — she is here | → *10 minutes ago* = specific time |

**Pair 4: Unfinished vs Finished**
| Present Perfect | Simple Past |
|----------------|------------|
| *"We **haven't finished** yet."* | *"We **didn't finish** the project **last Monday**."* |
| → Still not done — situation is ongoing | → Specific time (last Monday) = Simple Past |

---

### 🚨 The #1 Error — O Erro Mais Comum

> The biggest mistake Brazilian students make is **combining Present Perfect with a specific time marker**.

| ❌ WRONG | ✅ CORRECT | Rule |
|---------|-----------|------|
| *"I **have seen** him **yesterday**."* | *"I **saw** him **yesterday**."* | *Yesterday* → Simple Past |
| *"She **has called** me **at 3pm**."* | *"She **called** me **at 3pm**."* | Specific clock time → Simple Past |
| *"We **have met** **last year**."* | *"We **met last year**."* | *Last year* → Simple Past |
| *"**Have** you **gone** to the beach **in 2020**?"* | *"**Did** you **go** to the beach **in 2020**?"* | *In 2020* → Simple Past |

> 💡 **Memory Tip — Dica de memória:**  
> **Specific time = Simple Past. No time (or present-connected time) = Present Perfect.**  
> *Se você sabe QUANDO, use Simple Past. Se não importa quando (ou se ainda é relevante agora), use Present Perfect.*

---

**[Teacher Note: This slide is the most important conceptual slide of the lesson. Spend extra time here. The side-by-side pairs work best when you say both sentences aloud and ask students: "Do you notice a difference?" Let them guess the rule before you reveal it. The #1 error table should be reviewed carefully — ask students to identify the problem in each incorrect sentence before looking at the correction column. A useful analogy for Brazilian students: Em português, "Eu fui a Paris em 2018" e "Eu já fui a Paris" usam a mesma forma verbal. Em inglês, elas são diferentes: a primeira usa Simple Past (específico), a segunda usa Present Perfect (experiência geral). This click of understanding is often a breakthrough moment for students at this level.]**

## Slide 7: Already, Yet, Just 🔍
### Posição e Uso — Position & Meaning

---

### ✅ ALREADY — *já* (com ideia de que aconteceu antes do esperado)

**Position:** After *have/has* — **BEFORE** the main verb  
**Use:** Affirmative sentences + questions (when something happened sooner than expected)

| Example | Translation |
|---------|------------|
| *"I have **already** seen that film."* | *"Eu já vi esse filme."* |
| *"She has **already** finished her homework."* | *"Ela já terminou a lição de casa."* |
| *"Have you **already** eaten?"* (surprised) | *"Você já comeu?! (tão cedo assim?)"* |

> ⚠️ In informal spoken English, *already* can also appear at the end:  
> *"I've done it already!"* — but middle position is more standard.

---

### ✅ YET — *ainda* (negativas) / *já* (perguntas)

**Position:** END of the sentence  
**Use:** Negative sentences (something hasn't happened but is expected) + Questions

| Example | Translation |
|---------|------------|
| *"I haven't finished **yet**."* | *"Eu ainda não terminei."* |
| *"She hasn't called me **yet**."* | *"Ela ainda não me ligou."* |
| *"Have you eaten **yet**?"* | *"Você já comeu?"* |
| *"Has he arrived **yet**?"* | *"Ele já chegou?"* |

---

### ✅ JUST — *acabou de* / *há pouco*

**Position:** After *have/has* — **BEFORE** the main verb  
**Use:** Something happened very recently — moments or minutes ago

| Example | Translation |
|---------|------------|
| *"She has **just** called."* | *"Ela acabou de ligar."* |
| *"I've **just** arrived. Give me a minute!"* | *"Acabei de chegar. Me dá um minuto!"* |
| *"They've **just** announced the results."* | *"Eles acabaram de anunciar os resultados."* |

---

### 📊 Summary Table — Tabela Resumo

| Word | Position | Used in | Meaning | Example |
|------|----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **already** | after have/has (before main verb) | affirmative / questions | before expected — *antes do esperado* | *"I've already eaten."* |
| **yet** | end of sentence | negative / questions | expected but not happened — *esperado, mas ainda não* | *"I haven't eaten yet."* |
| **just** | after have/has (before main verb) | affirmative | very recently — *há pouco, agora mesmo* | *"I've just eaten."* |

---

### ❌ Common Errors — Erros com Already, Yet, Just

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Error Type |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| *"I **yet** haven't finished."* | *"I haven't finished **yet**."* | *yet* must go to the END |
| *"Have you already eaten **yet**?"* | *"Have you eaten **yet**?"* | Don't use *already* and *yet* together |
| *"She has **just** arrived **ago**."* | *"She has **just** arrived."* | *just* already implies recency — no need for *ago* |
| *"I **already** didn't go."* | *"I **haven't** gone **yet**."* | *already* is not used in negatives |

---

**[Teacher Note: Have students physically point to where each word goes in a sentence as you drill them. Use the board or write on paper: "I have ___ finished ___" and ask where *already* goes (middle) and where *yet* goes (end). Then practice with physical flashcards or verbal drills: say a sentence incomplete and have students shout the missing word and its position. Example: "I haven't called her ___" → students say "yet! At the end!" This kinesthetic approach helps students remember position rules. Confirm understanding before moving on — this is a frequent source of errors in writing.]**

## Slide 8: Recently, So Far, Up to Now 🕐
### Expressões de Tempo com Present Perfect

---

### ✅ RECENTLY — *recentemente*

**Position:** Beginning OR end of sentence  
**Meaning:** In the near past — not long ago  
**Use:** Present Perfect (or Simple Past in informal contexts)

| Example | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| *"I have **recently** started going to the gym."* | *recently* = middle position (more formal) |
| *"I've joined a cooking class **recently**."* | *recently* = end position |
| *"**Recently**, I've been thinking about changing jobs."* | *recently* = beginning — common in spoken English |

🗣️ **Conversation:**
> *"What have you been up to **recently**?"*  
> *"**Recently**, I've been learning to play guitar. I've **recently** downloaded a new app for it."*

---

### ✅ SO FAR — *até agora / até o momento*

**Position:** Beginning OR end of sentence  
**Meaning:** From the beginning of a period until this moment — emphasizes progress  
**Use:** Almost exclusively with Present Perfect

| Example | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| *"**So far**, I have studied three units."* | Beginning — common |
| *"I've learned about 100 new words **so far**."* | End — also common |
| *"**So far**, everything has gone well."* | Great for updates/progress reports |

🗣️ **Conversation:**
> *"How's the new job going?"*  
> *"**So far, so good!** I've met the team and I've already finished my first project."*

> 💡 **Idiom:** *"So far, so good"* = Everything is fine until now. Extremely common in everyday English!

---

### ✅ UP TO NOW — *até agora / até este momento*

**Position:** Usually at the beginning of the sentence  
**Meaning:** Everything from the past until this exact moment — often implies things may change  
**Use:** Present Perfect — formal and emphatic

| Example | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| *"**Up to now**, I have never missed a class."* | Implies: I've been perfect, but who knows about the future... |
| *"**Up to now**, the project has been on schedule."* | Good for formal updates / presentations |
| *"**Up to now**, we haven't had any technical problems."* | Emphasizes the entire period so far |

🗣️ **Conversation:**
> *"How long have you been studying English?"*  
> *"**Up to now**, I've been studying for about two years. So far, I've learned a lot, but I still haven't mastered the pronunciation **yet**!"*

---

### 📊 Comparison — Comparação

| Expression | Focus | Typical Use |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| **recently** | Time period: recent past | Casual and formal — talking about what's new |
| **so far** | Progress: from start to now | Progress reports, updates, conversations about ongoing situations |
| **up to now** | Completeness: the entire period | Formal, emphatic — often implies possible change ahead |

---

**[Teacher Note: These three expressions are often overlooked in traditional grammar teaching but are extremely common in natural spoken and written English. Point out that *so far* is conversational gold — students can use it in emails, presentations, and everyday chat. Practice a round of "life updates" using all three: have each student say one sentence about their English studies using *recently*, *so far*, and *up to now*. Example prompt: "Tell us about your experience learning English recently / so far / up to now." This also doubles as a speaking warm-down before the structured activities in Slides 9-11.]**

## Slide 9: "Have you ever...?" — Complete Structure 🗣️
### Perguntando sobre Experiências de Vida

---

### 🔧 Formation — Formação

```
Have / Has  +  subject  +  EVER  +  past participle  +  ?
```

| Question | Translation |
|----------|------------|
| *"**Have you ever** been to Japan?"* | *"Você já foi ao Japão alguma vez?"* |
| *"**Has she ever** tried surfing?"* | *"Ela já tentou surfar alguma vez?"* |
| *"**Have they ever** eaten Brazilian food?"* | *"Eles já comeram comida brasileira?"* |
| *"**Has he ever** won a competition?"* | *"Ele já ganhou alguma competição?"* |

---

### ✅ Affirmative Answers — Respostas Afirmativas

> **Formula: Yes, I have. + [Simple Past for details]**

| Question | Affirmative Answer |
|----------|-------------------|
| *"Have you ever been to Japan?"* | *"Yes, I **have**! I **went** there in 2019 with my family."* |
| *"Has she ever tried surfing?"* | *"Yes, she **has**. She **tried** it last summer in Florianópolis."* |

---

### ❌ Negative Answers — Respostas Negativas

> **Formula: No, I haven't. I've never + [past participle]**

| Question | Negative Answer |
|----------|----------------|
| *"Have you ever been to Japan?"* | *"No, I **haven't**. I've **never** been to Asia."* |
| *"Have you ever eaten sushi?"* | *"No, I **haven't** — I've **never** tried it, but I'd like to!"* |

---

### 📋 6 Example Questions + Answers

| # | Question | ✅ Yes Answer | ❌ No Answer |
|---|----------|--------------|------------|
| 1 | *"Have you ever lived in another city?"* | *"Yes, I have! I lived in Recife for 3 years."* | *"No, I haven't. I've always lived here."* |
| 2 | *"Have you ever spoken English with a foreigner?"* | *"Yes! I spoke with a tourist last year."* | *"No, never — but I'd love to try!"* |
| 3 | *"Have you ever cooked a new dish?"* | *"Yes! I made pasta from scratch last week."* | *"No, I haven't. I've never cooked anything fancy."* |
| 4 | *"Have you ever watched a film in English without subtitles?"* | *"Yes, once! I watched Toy Story and understood most of it."* | *"No, not yet. My English isn't that advanced yet."* |
| 5 | *"Have you ever done something you were really scared of?"* | *"Yes! I jumped off a cliff into water in 2021. I was terrified!"* | *"No, I haven't. I'm quite cautious!"* |
| 6 | *"Have you ever won something important?"* | *"Yes! I won a chess tournament when I was 12."* | *"No, I've never won anything big. Maybe someday!"* |

---

### 🔄 Follow-Up Questions in Simple Past

When the answer is "Yes, I have" → **Ask for details in Simple Past!**

| Follow-up Question | Why Simple Past? |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| *"**When did** you go?"* | Specific past time → SP |
| *"**Where did** you stay?"* | Specific location in the past → SP |
| *"**Who did** you go with?"* | Specific people in the past → SP |
| *"**How long did** you stay?"* | Completed duration → SP |
| *"**What did** you think of it?"* | Opinion formed in the past → SP |

## Slide 10: Guided Practice — Part 1 ✏️
### Exercícios em Aula

---

### 📝 Exercise A: Present Perfect or Simple Past?
**Choose the correct form for each sentence. Circule a forma correta.**

1. *"I __________ (have visited / visited) London three times in my life."*
2. *"We __________ (have seen / saw) that movie last Tuesday at the cinema."*
3. *"__________ (Have you ever tried / Did you ever try) Vietnamese food?"*
4. *"She __________ (has called / called) me at 7pm yesterday."*
5. *"They __________ (have just arrived / just arrived) — they're at the door right now!"*

---

### 📝 Exercise B: Insert Already, Yet, or Just
**Rewrite each sentence, placing the adverb in the correct position.**

1. *"I have finished my coffee."* → Insert **already**
2. *"Have you spoken to the teacher?"* → Insert **yet**
3. *"She has sent the email."* → Insert **just**

---

### ✅ Answer Key — Gabarito

**Exercise A:**
1. *"I **have visited** London three times in my life."* → No specific time = PP ✓
2. *"We **saw** that movie last Tuesday."* → *last Tuesday* = SP ✓
3. *"**Have you ever tried** Vietnamese food?"* → Life experience = PP ✓
4. *"She **called** me at 7pm yesterday."* → *yesterday / at 7pm* = SP ✓
5. *"They **have just arrived**."* → *just* with present result = PP ✓

**Exercise B:**
1. *"I have **already** finished my coffee."* → after *have*, before *finished* ✓
2. *"Have you spoken to the teacher **yet**?"* → end of sentence ✓
3. *"She has **just** sent the email."* → after *has*, before *sent* ✓

---

### ⚠️ Exercise C: Spot the Error
**Find and correct the mistake in each sentence.**

1. *"I have seen him yesterday at the park."*
2. *"Did you ever try Thai food in your life?"*
3. *"She has went to Paris last year."*

**Corrections:**
1. → *"I **saw** him yesterday at the park."* (*yesterday* → SP)
2. → *"**Have you ever tried** Thai food?"* (life experience → PP)
3. → *"She **went** to Paris last year."* (*last year* + wrong participle → SP)

---

**[Teacher Note: Do exercises A and B together as a class first (questions 1–2), then have students complete the rest in pairs. Exercise C works best as a quick-fire group activity — read the incorrect sentence aloud and have students call out the correction. This "error hunt" format is motivating and builds proofreading awareness. Approximate timing: 10–12 minutes for all three exercises with brief discussion. Collect common errors you notice during pair work for collective correction after Slide 11. Avoid singling out individual students for mistakes — frame corrections as "a common challenge" for the whole group.]**

## Slide 11: Guided Practice — Part 2 🎤
### Life Experiences Interview — Entrevista de Experiências de Vida

---

### 🎯 Speaking Activity: Pair Interview

**Setup:** Work in pairs. Partner A interviews Partner B for 5 minutes, then switch.

---

**📋 Instructions for Partner A — Entrevistador:**

1. Choose **5 different topics** from the list below
2. Ask: *"Have you ever...?"* using a topic
3. If Partner B says **"Yes, I have"** → ask **2 follow-up questions in Simple Past**
4. Take notes on Partner B's most interesting experience

**📋 Instructions for Partner B — Entrevistado:**

1. Answer honestly using Present Perfect: *"Yes, I have"* / *"No, I haven't / I've never..."*
2. If yes: give details using Simple Past
3. Use at least **one time expression** (*recently, just, already, so far, up to now*)

---

### 💬 Model Conversation — Conversa Modelo

> **Partner A:** *"Have you ever traveled abroad?"*  
> **Partner B:** *"Yes, I have! I've actually been to Argentina twice."*  
> **Partner A:** *"Really! When did you first go?"*  
> **Partner B:** *"I went the first time in 2019 with some friends."*  
> **Partner A:** *"And what did you like most about it?"*  
> **Partner B:** *"The food was incredible! I had the best steak of my life there. Have you ever been?"*  
> **Partner A:** *"No, I haven't, but I've always wanted to go. So far, I've only traveled within Brazil."*

**Notice:**
- ✅ PP for the experience: *"I've been to Argentina"*
- ✅ SP for the details: *"I went in 2019", "I had the best steak"*
- ✅ Natural follow-up questions in SP: *"When did you go?", "What did you like?"*
- ✅ Time expression naturally included: *"So far, I've only traveled within Brazil"*

---

### 🔄 Share with the Group
After the pair activity, each pair shares:
> *"The most interesting experience [Partner's name] has had is..."*  
> (in Present Perfect, then add details in Simple Past)

---

**[Teacher Note: This is the communicative peak of the lesson — the moment students use everything they've learned in real conversation. Circulate during the pair activity and listen carefully. Your job at this stage is NOT to correct every error but to note the most frequent or important ones for collective feedback at the end. Note especially: (1) Do students switch naturally from PP to SP for details? (2) Are they using follow-up questions in SP correctly? (3) Are any time expressions (already, yet, just, so far) coming up organically? After the pairs share with the group, take 3–4 minutes to review the 2–3 most common errors you noticed, framing them as "Let's look at something interesting that came up" rather than correcting anyone directly. This creates a safe, positive learning environment.]**

## Slide 12: Wrap-Up & Homework 🏠
### Resumo e Tarefas para Casa

---

### ✅ What We Learned Today — O que aprendemos hoje

✅ **Present Perfect — Formation**  
→ *have/has + past participle* in affirmative, negative, and question forms  
→ Contractions: *I've, She's, They've...*

✅ **Present Perfect — Main Uses**  
→ Life experiences with no specific time  
→ Recent actions with present relevance  
→ With time adverbs: *already, yet, just, recently, so far, up to now*

✅ **Present Perfect vs Simple Past**  
→ Specific time marker → Simple Past  
→ No specific time / present connection → Present Perfect

✅ **Already, Yet, Just — Position and Meaning**  
→ *Already* and *Just* → after *have/has*, before main verb  
→ *Yet* → end of sentence (negatives and questions)

✅ **Recently, So Far, Up to Now**  
→ Flexible position (beginning/end) with Present Perfect  
→ Natural, conversational — great for progress updates

✅ **"Have you ever...?" Conversations**  
→ PP for the question → SP for the details  
→ Follow-up questions: *When did you...? Where did you...? Who did you go with?*

### 📚 Homework — Tarefas de Casa

---

**📝 Task 1 — Writing (Escrita)**

Write a paragraph of **60–80 words** about:
> *"The most interesting experience I have ever had"*

**Requirements:**
- Use **Present Perfect** to introduce the experience
- Use **Simple Past** for the details (when, where, who, what happened)
- Include **at least 2** of these words: *already, yet, just, recently, so far, never, ever*
- Example opening: *"The most interesting experience I have ever had was..."*

---

**🔄 Task 2 — Transformation (Transformação)**

Rewrite each sentence using the correct tense. Escolha PP ou Simple Past:

1. *"She visited Rome. It was in 2020."* → Make one PP sentence + one SP sentence
2. *"I never tried Korean food."* → Use Present Perfect
3. *"Did he ever go to the mountains? He went last December."* → Convert the question to PP
4. *"They finished the project. They finished it two days ago."* → Choose the correct tense
5. *"We already saw that show. We saw it on Sunday."* → Fix the error and rewrite correctly

---

**🔍 Task 3 — Find it in the Wild! (Pesquisa)**

Find **one sentence with Present Perfect** in English from:
- A TV series / film subtitle
- A song lyric
- A website, app, or social media post
- A book or article

Write down:
1. The sentence you found
2. Where you found it
3. Why is Present Perfect used here? (*Experience? Recent action? With already/yet/just?*)
