## 📚 2. Grammar Focus

### 2.1 Comparative Adjectives — *Adjetivos Comparativos*

Use comparatives to compare **two things**.

**Regras de formação:**

| Adjective Type | Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Short (1 syllable) | add **-er** | fast → **faster** |
| Short ending in -e | add **-r** | nice → **nicer** |
| Short ending in CVC | double consonant + **-er** | big → **bigger** |
| 2+ syllables | use **more / less** | expensive → **more expensive** |
| Irregular | memorize! | good → **better** / bad → **worse** |

**Structure:** Subject + verb + **comparative** + **than** + noun/pronoun

✅ **Examples:**
- The new phone is **faster than** the old one.
- This restaurant is **more expensive than** the one downtown.
- Electric cars are **better than** gas cars for the environment.
- The test was **less difficult than** I expected.
- Today's lesson is **worse than** yesterday's — just kidding! 😄

**⚠️ Common Mistakes:**

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct |
|---|---|
| She is more tall than me. | She is **taller** than me. |
| This is more good than that. | This is **better** than that. |
| He runs more fast than her. | He runs **faster** than her. |
| It is the more expensive. | It is **more expensive** (than the others). |

---

### 2.2 Superlative Adjectives — *Adjetivos Superlativos*

Use superlatives to say something is the **most extreme** in a group (3 or more).

**Regras de formação:**

| Adjective Type | Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Short (1 syllable) | **the** + adj + **-est** | fast → **the fastest** |
| Short ending in -e | **the** + adj + **-st** | nice → **the nicest** |
| Short ending in CVC | **the** + double consonant + **-est** | big → **the biggest** |
| 2+ syllables | **the most / the least** | expensive → **the most expensive** |
| Irregular | memorize! | good → **the best** / bad → **the worst** |

**Structure:** Subject + verb + **the** + superlative (+ noun)

✅ **Examples:**
- Tokyo is **the most expensive** city I've ever visited.
- That was **the worst** movie I've ever seen!
- Summer is **the hottest** season of the year.
- She is **the best** student in our class.
- This is **the least interesting** book on the shelf.

**💡 Comparative vs. Superlative at a glance:**

| | Comparative (2 things) | Superlative (3+ things) |
|---|---|---|
| **short adj** | tall → taller **than** | tall → **the** tallest |
| **long adj** | beautiful → more beautiful **than** | beautiful → **the most** beautiful |
| **good** | better **than** | **the best** |
| **bad** | worse **than** | **the worst** |
| **far** | farther/further **than** | **the farthest/furthest** |

---

### 2.3 "As...as" Constructions — *Tão...quanto*

Use **as + adjective/adverb + as** to say two things are **equal** — or **not as...as** to say they are unequal.

**Structures:**

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **as + adj + as** | equally / the same | She is **as tall as** her brother. |
| **not as + adj + as** | less than | This film is **not as good as** the book. |
| **just as + adj + as** | emphasizes equality | My coffee is **just as hot as** yours. |
| **twice as + adj + as** | double | He earns **twice as much as** I do. |

✅ **More Examples:**
- São Paulo is **as busy as** New York.
- The new model is **not as reliable as** the previous one.
- Learning Spanish is **not as difficult as** learning Chinese.
- Your cake is **just as delicious as** the bakery's!

---

### 2.4 Contrast Words & Connectors — *Palavras de Contraste*

Use these words to express **contrast** between two ideas:

| Connector | Position | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **although / even though** | beginning or middle of sentence | **Although** it was raining, we went out. |
| **whereas** | middle of sentence (joins two clauses) | I like tea, **whereas** she prefers coffee. |
| **unlike** | before a noun/pronoun | **Unlike** me, he loves early mornings. |
| **however** | beginning of new sentence (+ comma) | The hotel was beautiful. **However**, it was very noisy. |

✅ **Examples in context:**
- **Although** the restaurant was expensive, the food was worth it.
- Brazil is hot and tropical, **whereas** Canada is cold and snowy.
- **Unlike** public transport, driving gives you more freedom.
- I studied all night. **However**, I still failed the exam.

> ⚠️ **Note:** *Although* and *whereas* connect two clauses in **one sentence**. *However* usually starts a **new sentence** (or comes after a semicolon).

*Read the text below carefully, then answer the questions.*

### City Life vs. Country Life

Many people debate whether it is better to live in a city or in the countryside. City life is generally **faster** and **more stressful** than life in a rural area. Cities offer **the most** job opportunities and the best access to entertainment, shopping, and healthcare. **However**, they are also **noisier** and **more expensive** than rural areas.

Country life, on the other hand, is **not as hectic as** city life. The air is cleaner, the pace is slower, and communities are often **closer** and **more connected**. **Unlike** city dwellers, people in rural areas usually know their neighbors well. **Although** there are fewer job options, many people find country life more fulfilling.

In the end, neither is **the best** choice for everyone — it depends on your priorities and lifestyle.

*(148 words)*

4. What does the expression *"hectic"* mean in the context of the text? (Use context clues!)

## ✍️ 4. Writing Activity

*Exercício guiado de produção escrita*

### A: The Comparison Paragraph

Choose **two things** from the list below (or your own choice!) and write a **comparison paragraph** of 8–10 sentences.

**Choose two:**

```

[Topic sentence] ______________ and ______________ are two [nouns] that have 
both similarities and differences.

[Similarity] They are similar because __________________________________.
Also, both ______________ and ______________ are _________________________.

[Contrast 1] However, ______________ is more ______________ than ______________.
Unlike ______________, ______________ is ______________________________________.

[Contrast 2] Although ______________________________________________, ________
____________________________________________________________________________.

[Superlative] In my opinion, ______________ is the most ______________ because
____________________________________________________________________________.

[Conclusion] Whereas _________________________, ______________________________.
Overall, I prefer ______________ because _____________________________________.
```

### What to Listen For:

While you listen, pay attention to:

- ✅ **Comparative adjectives** — which things are being compared?
