---

### 📝 Vocabulary Notes / Notas de Vocabulário

> **💡 WHILE vs WHEN — A diferença crucial:**
>
> - **WHILE** → normalmente acompanha **Past Continuous** → *while + was/were + -ing*
>   - *While I **was studying**, my dog started barking.*
>
> - **WHEN** → normalmente acompanha **Simple Past** → *when + verb no passado simples*
>   - *When my dog **started** barking, I was studying.*
>
> 🔁 **As duas frases acima têm o mesmo significado!** A estrutura é que muda.

---

> **💡 MEANWHILE vs WHILE:**
>
> - **WHILE** → une duas ações NA MESMA frase: *While she was cooking, he arrived.*
> - **MEANWHILE** → separa duas ações em frases diferentes: *She was cooking. Meanwhile, he was setting the table.*

# PART 2 — GRAMMAR / GRAMÁTICA

## 📐 Structure 1: Past Continuous — Formation
### *(Formação do Past Continuous)*

---

### The Formula / A Fórmula:

```
Subject + WAS / WERE + Verb-ING
```

> **Quando usar WAS vs WERE?**
> - **WAS** → I, He, She, It
> - **WERE** → You, We, They

---

### 📊 Table 1: Past Continuous — Affirmative, Negative & Interrogative

| Form | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Affirmative (+)** | Subject + *was/were* + verb-ing | *She **was sleeping** at 9pm.* |
| **Negative (-)** | Subject + *wasn't/weren't* + verb-ing | *They **weren't watching** TV.* |
| **Yes/No Question (?)** | *Was/Were* + subject + verb-ing? | ***Were** you **studying**?* |
| **WH- Question** | *Wh-word + was/were* + subject + verb-ing? | *What **were** you **doing**?* |

---

### ✏️ Spelling Rules for VERB + ING / Regras de Ortografia

| Rule | When to use | Base Form → -ING |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Just add -ing** | Most regular verbs | *work → **working*** / *study → **studying*** |
| **Drop the -e, add -ing** | Verbs ending in silent -e | *drive → **driving*** / *write → **writing*** |
| **Double the consonant** | Short verb ending in vowel + consonant | *run → **running*** / *sit → **sitting*** |
| **Change -ie to -y** | Verbs ending in -ie | *lie → **lying*** / *die → **dying*** |

---

> ⚠️ **Common Mistakes / Erros Comuns:**
>
> | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
> |----------|-----------|
> | She was *drving* | She was **driving** |
> | He was *runing* | He was **running** |
> | They were *studing* | They were **studying** |
> | I was *makeing* | I was **making** |

## 📐 Structure 2: Past Continuous vs Simple Past
### *(Contraste: Past Continuous x Simple Past)*

---

> **The KEY difference / A diferença CHAVE:**
>
> - **Past Continuous** → ação longa, em progresso (the background / o cenário de fundo)
> - **Simple Past** → ação curta, pontual, que interrompe (the interruption / a interrupção)

---

### 📊 Table 2: Contrast — Past Continuous vs Simple Past

| Past Continuous | Simple Past | What it means |
|----------------|-------------|---------------|
| *I **was cooking***... | *...when you **called**.* | Cooking = in progress; calling = interruption |
| *She **was walking***... | *...when it **started** to rain.* | Walking = background; rain = sudden event |
| *They **were sleeping***... | *...when the alarm **went off**.* | Sleeping = ongoing; alarm = interruption |

---

### 🗓️ Timeline Visualization / Visualização na Linha do Tempo

```
Past ←————————————————————————————————→ Present

     [=========== WAS COOKING ===========]
                         ↑
                    phone CALLED
                   (Simple Past)

       "I WAS COOKING when the phone RANG."
```

```
Past ←————————————————————————————————→ Present

     [===== WAS SLEEPING =====]  [=====still sleeping=====]
                    ↑
              alarm WENT OFF
             (Simple Past)

    "She WAS SLEEPING when the alarm WENT OFF."
```

---

### 📊 Table 3: Patterns with WHILE and WHEN

| Pattern | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **While + Past Continuous, Simple Past** | While + [was/were -ing], [verb-ed] | *While I was reading, she **arrived**.* |
| **Simple Past + when + Past Continuous** | [verb-ed] + when + [was/were -ing] | *She **arrived** when I was reading.* |
| **While + PC, PC (simultaneous)** | While + [was -ing], [was -ing] | *While he cooked, she cleaned.* |
| **Simple Past, meanwhile + Past Continuous** | [verb-ed]. Meanwhile, [was -ing]. | *He cooked. Meanwhile, she was cleaning.* |

---

> **💡 Remember / Lembre-se:**
> - *While I was cooking* **=** *When the fire alarm went off, I was cooking*
> - As frases têm o **mesmo significado** — só a estrutura muda!

# PART 4 — GRAMMAR REFERENCE / REFERÊNCIA GRAMATICAL

## 📚 Quick Reference Guide

*(Keep this section for study at home! / Guarde esta seção para estudar em casa!)*

---

### Past Continuous — Complete Summary

#### When to Use / Quando Usar:

| Situation | Signal Words | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Action in progress at a specific past time | *at 8pm, at that time, then* | *At midnight, they were still dancing.* |
| Action interrupted by another | *when + Simple Past* | *I was sleeping when you called.* |
| Two simultaneous past actions | *while, meanwhile* | *While he cooked, she read.* |
| Setting the scene in a narrative | *It was..., There were...* | *It was raining and everyone was running.* |

---

#### ⚠️ State Verbs — NOT usually used in Continuous
*(Verbos de estado — geralmente NÃO usados no contínuo)*

> These verbs describe **states**, not actions, so they usually DON'T use -ing:
> *(Esses verbos descrevem **estados**, não ações, então geralmente NÃO usam -ing)*

| Category | Verbs |
|----------|-------|
| Emotions / Sentimentos | *love, hate, like, prefer, want, need* |
| Mental states / Estados mentais | *know, understand, believe, remember, think (opinion)* |
| Senses / Sentidos | *see, hear, smell, taste, feel (= believe)* |
| Possession / Posse | *have (possession), own, belong* |

> ❌ *She was knowing the answer.* → ✅ *She **knew** the answer.*
> ❌ *I was wanting more coffee.* → ✅ *I **wanted** more coffee.*

---

### Key Patterns Summary / Resumo dos Padrões

```
Pattern 1: WHILE + [was/were -ing] + , + [Simple Past]
→ While I was sleeping, the phone rang.

Pattern 2: [Simple Past] + WHEN + [was/were -ing]
→ The phone rang when I was sleeping.

Pattern 3: [was/were -ing] + WHEN + [Simple Past]
→ I was sleeping when the phone rang.

Pattern 4: [was/were -ing]. MEANWHILE, [was/were -ing].
→ She was cooking. Meanwhile, he was setting the table.
```
