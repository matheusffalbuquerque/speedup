## SLIDE 2 — Warm-up: Story Chain Game (10 min)

### 🎮 Warm-up: Story Chain Game

**Objetivo:** Ativar uso do Past Continuous de forma criativa e divertida.

---

#### ➤ Como jogar — Instruções passo a passo:

**Passo 1 — Setup (1 min):**
- Peça aos alunos que se sentem em círculo (ou em semicírculo se for turma pequena).
- Diga: *"We're going to create a story together! Each person adds one sentence."*

**Passo 2 — Frase inicial do professor (modelo):**
> *"It was a stormy night. The wind was blowing hard..."*

**Passo 3 — Aluno 1 continua:**
> *(Exemplo esperado):* *"...and a woman was walking alone in the street..."*

**Passo 4 — Aluno 2 adiciona interrupção:**
> *(Exemplo esperado):* *"...when suddenly her phone rang."*

**Passo 5 — Continua até cada aluno ter falado pelo menos 2 vezes.**

---

#### ➤ Regras do jogo:
1. Cada frase **deve fazer sentido** com a anterior.
2. Tente usar **Past Continuous** quando possível.
3. Se um aluno travar, o professor dá uma dica com um cartão de palavra-gatilho:
   - *"Meanwhile..."* / *"Suddenly..."* / *"While this was happening..."*

---

#### ➤ Frases de apoio para o professor (use se necessário):
| Gatilho | Sugestão |
|---------|----------|
| *Meanwhile...* | *"Meanwhile, inside the house, the family was eating dinner."* |
| *Suddenly...* | *"Suddenly, the lights went out."* |
| *At that moment...* | *"At that moment, someone was climbing the stairs."* |

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> Não corrija erros gramaticais durante o jogo — o objetivo é fluência e confiança. Anote os erros mais comuns para revisar nos slides de gramática. Ao final do warm-up, valorize a criatividade: *"What an amazing story! Now let's learn to tell stories like this perfectly."*

## SLIDE 4 — Contexto Comunicativo

### 🌍 Why do we need Past Continuous?

---

#### Imagine this situation:

> *"You arrive late to a party. Your friend asks: 'Where were you? What were you doing?'"*
>
> ✅ *"I was driving home when I got stuck in traffic."*
> ✅ *"My sister was calling me when I was trying to leave."*
> ✅ *"While I was getting ready, my car broke down!"*

---

#### When do we use it?

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Action in progress in the past** | *She was sleeping at 10pm.* |
| **Interrupted action** | *I was cooking when you called.* |
| **Two simultaneous past actions** | *While he was studying, she was watching TV.* |
| **Setting the scene in a story** | *It was raining. People were running...* |

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> Use o exemplo da festa como roleplay rápido: pergunte a um aluno *"You arrived late to class today — what were you doing?"* e incentive respostas com Past Continuous. Isso cria conexão com a vida real antes de entrar na gramática formal.

## SLIDE 5 — Vocabulário (Parte 1)

### 📚 Vocabulary: Key Words for This Lesson

---

#### Group 1 — Time Connectors (Conectores de Tempo)

| Word | Translation | Example Sentence |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **while** | enquanto | *While I was studying, my dog started barking.* |
| **when** | quando | *When she arrived, we were having dinner.* |
| **during** | durante | *He fell asleep during the movie.* |
| **meanwhile** | enquanto isso / nesse meio tempo | *She was cooking. Meanwhile, he was setting the table.* |

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> Destaque a diferença entre **while** e **when**:
> - **While** → geralmente acompanha Past Continuous (*while + was/were + -ing*)
> - **When** → geralmente acompanha Simple Past (*when + verb no passado simples*)
> Escreva os dois padrões no quadro:
> - *While I WAS COOKING, the phone RANG.*
> - *When the phone RANG, I WAS COOKING.*
> Eles dizem a mesma coisa! Explore isso com os alunos.

## SLIDE 7 — Gramática: Formação do Past Continuous

### 📐 Grammar: Past Continuous — Formation

---

#### Formula / Fórmula:

```
Subject + WAS / WERE + Verb-ING
```

---

#### Affirmative ✅

| Subject | Auxiliary | Verb-ing | Example |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| I | **was** | working | *I was working at 8pm.* |
| You | **were** | studying | *You were studying all night.* |
| He / She / It | **was** | sleeping | *She was sleeping when I called.* |
| We / They | **were** | playing | *They were playing football.* |

---

#### Negative ❌

```
Subject + WAS NOT (wasn't) / WERE NOT (weren't) + Verb-ING
```

| Example |
|---------|
| *I wasn't watching TV — I was reading.* |
| *They weren't sleeping; they were talking.* |

---

#### Interrogative ❓

```
WAS / WERE + Subject + Verb-ING ?
```

| Example | Short Answer |
|---------|-------------|
| *Was she working?* | *Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.* |
| *Were they listening?* | *Yes, they were. / No, they weren't.* |
| *What were you doing?* | *I was cooking dinner.* |

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> Escreva a fórmula no quadro e deixe visível durante toda a aula. Peça aos alunos que conjuguem verbos do warm-up (Story Chain Game) usando a tabela. Exemplo: *"In our story, what WAS the woman DOING?"* → *"She was walking."*

## SLIDE 8 — Gramática: Spelling Rules (-ing)

### ✏️ Spelling Rules for Verb + ING

---

| Rule | When to apply | Examples |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Just add -ing** | Most verbs | *work → working*, *study → studying*, *sleep → sleeping* |
| **Drop final -e, then add -ing** | Verbs ending in silent -e | *drive → driving*, *write → writing*, *make → making* |
| **Double the final consonant** | Short verb + stressed vowel + single consonant | *run → running*, *sit → sitting*, *stop → stopping* |
| **Change -ie to -y, then add -ing** | Verbs ending in -ie | *lie → lying*, *die → dying* |

---

#### ⚠️ Common Mistakes:
- ❌ *drving* → ✅ *driving*
- ❌ *runing* → ✅ *running*
- ❌ *studing* → ✅ *studying*

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> Faça um mini quiz rápido: escreva verbos no quadro e peça aos alunos que escrevam a forma -ing. Verbos sugeridos: *swim, come, begin, enjoy, travel*. Corrija junto com a turma.

## SLIDE 9 — Gramática: Past Continuous vs Simple Past

### ⚖️ Past Continuous vs Simple Past — The Contrast

---

#### Key Concept / Conceito-Chave:

> **Past Continuous** = ação longa, em progresso
> **Simple Past** = ação curta que interrompe (ou aconteceu em um ponto específico)

---

#### Timeline Visual:

```
Past ←————————————————————————————→ Present

         [========= COOKING =========]
                         ↑
                     phone RANG
                    (Simple Past)

"I WAS COOKING when the phone RANG."
```

---

#### Compare:

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| *I **was reading** when he **called**.* | Reading = in progress; calling = interruption |
| *While she **was walking**, it **started** to rain.* | Walking = background; rain = sudden event |
| *They **were watching** TV and **eating** pizza.* | Two simultaneous ongoing actions |
| *He **arrived** at 8. We **were waiting** for him.* | Arrived = point in time; waiting = ongoing before |

---

#### Pattern Summary:

```
While + [Past Continuous] , [Simple Past]
[Simple Past] + when + [Past Continuous]
```

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> Desenhe a linha do tempo no quadro — ela é muito eficaz visualmente. Pergunte: *"Which action is the 'background' and which is the 'interruption'?"* Isso ajuda os alunos a entenderem o contraste de forma intuitiva antes de decorar a regra.

---

## SLIDE 10 — Gramática: While vs When

### 🔗 Using WHILE and WHEN

---

#### WHILE → Past Continuous

```
WHILE + Subject + WAS/WERE + Verb-ING ...
```

> *While I was sleeping, someone knocked on the door.*
> *While they were arguing, the baby woke up.*
> *While she was driving, she saw an accident.*

---

#### WHEN → Simple Past

```
... WHEN + Subject + Verb (Simple Past)
```

> *I was sleeping when someone knocked on the door.*
> *They were arguing when the baby woke up.*
> *She was driving when she saw an accident.*

---

#### ⚡ SAME MEANING — Different structure!

| With WHILE | With WHEN |
|-----------|----------|
| *While I was reading, my phone rang.* | *My phone rang when I was reading.* |
| *While he was running, he fell.* | *He fell when he was running.* |

---

#### MEANWHILE → Separate action at the same time

> *John was cooking dinner. Meanwhile, Mary was setting the table.*
> *(Two separate people doing things at the same time)*

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> Destaque que **WHILE** e **WHEN** podem trocar de posição na frase sem mudar o significado. Escreva o par de frases no quadro e pergunte: *"Do these sentences mean the same thing?"* Espere a confirmação dos alunos antes de avançar.

## SLIDE 11 — Prática Guiada (Oral)

### 🗣️ Guided Practice: What Were You Doing?

---

#### Activity 1 — Personal Questions (5 min)

Ask each student and encourage full-sentence answers:

1. *"What were you doing yesterday at 7pm?"*
2. *"What were you doing this morning when you woke up?"*
3. *"What were you doing last Saturday afternoon?"*
4. *"Were you sleeping at midnight last night? If not, what were you doing?"*
5. *"What was your family doing when you left home today?"*

---

#### Activity 2 — Picture Description (5 min)

Describe this scene (or draw it on the board):

> 🖼️ *A busy café. A man was reading a newspaper. Two women were talking and laughing. A waiter was carrying coffee. A child was drawing on a napkin. Suddenly, a dog ran in through the door!*

Questions:
- *"What was the man doing?"*
- *"What were the two women doing?"*
- *"What happened suddenly?"*
- *"What do you think happened next?"*

---

#### Activity 3 — Pair Work: Alibi Game (5 min)

> 💼 **Scenario:** *A store was robbed yesterday between 6pm and 8pm. You need an alibi! Work with a partner. Ask and answer: "What were you doing at [time]?"*

Partner A asks → Partner B answers using Past Continuous.
Then switch!

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> No Alibi Game, incentive os alunos a criarem histórias detalhadas: *"I was having dinner with my family"* não é suficiente — pergunte: *"Where were you eating? Who was there? What were you talking about?"* Isso pratica o uso sustentado do Past Continuous.

## SLIDE 12 — Exercício em Sala 1: Fill in the Blank

### ✍️ In-Class Exercise 1: Complete the Sentences

**Instructions:** Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the verb in parentheses. Use **Past Continuous** or **Simple Past**.

---

1. I __________ (read) a book when my phone __________ (ring).
2. While she __________ (cook), her husband __________ (arrive) home.
3. They __________ (not / watch) TV — they __________ (play) cards.
4. __________ you __________ (sleep) when I called?
5. The children __________ (run) in the park when it __________ (start) to rain.
6. While we __________ (wait) for the bus, we __________ (see) a street performer.
7. He __________ (drive) to work when he __________ (have) an accident.
8. What __________ you __________ (do) at 10 o'clock last night?

---

**Answer Key (para o professor):**

| # | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 1 | *was reading / rang* |
| 2 | *was cooking / arrived* |
| 3 | *weren't watching / were playing* |
| 4 | *Were you sleeping* |
| 5 | *were running / started* |
| 6 | *were waiting / saw* |
| 7 | *was driving / had* |
| 8 | *were you doing* |

> Corrija os exercícios coletivamente — não dê as respostas antes que os alunos tentem. Peça que um aluno leia a frase completa em voz alta após cada correção para reforçar o padrão sonoro.

## SLIDE 13 — Exercício em Sala 2: Sentence Transformation

### ✍️ In-Class Exercise 2: Rewrite the Sentences

**Instructions:** Rewrite each sentence starting with the word given. Keep the same meaning.

---

1. *I was cooking when the doorbell rang.*
   → **When** ___________________________________

2. *When he called, she was studying.*
   → **While** ___________________________________

3. *They were arguing. At the same time, the kids were sleeping.*
   → **Meanwhile,** ___________________________________

4. *She was walking to school. It started to snow.*
   → **While** ___________________________________

5. *We were having a meeting when the fire alarm went off.*
   → **When** ___________________________________

---

**Answer Key:**

| # | Suggested Answer |
|---|-----------------|
| 1 | *When the doorbell rang, I was cooking.* |
| 2 | *While she was studying, he called.* |
| 3 | *They were arguing. Meanwhile, the kids were sleeping.* |
| 4 | *While she was walking to school, it started to snow.* |
| 5 | *When the fire alarm went off, we were having a meeting.* |

---

> 📋 **Nota para o professor:**
> Explore que a frase pode começar tanto com *while/when* quanto com o resultado — ambas estão corretas. Isso mostra flexibilidade na escrita. Bônus: peça aos alunos que leiam as frases em voz alta com entonação natural.

## SLIDE 17 — Extra: Grammar Bank (Referência rápida)

### 📚 Grammar Bank: Past Continuous at a Glance

---

#### Uses / Usos:

| Use | Signal Words | Example |
|-----|-------------|---------|
| Action in progress at a past moment | *at 8pm, at that time* | *At 8pm, I was watching TV.* |
| Interrupted past action | *when + Simple Past* | *I was sleeping when you called.* |
| Two actions at the same time | *while, meanwhile* | *While he cooked, she cleaned.* |
| Setting the scene (narratives) | *It was..., People were...* | *It was raining and everyone was running.* |
| Polite/tentative past questions | — | *I was wondering if you could help me.* |

---

#### ⚠️ Verbs NOT usually used in Continuous (State Verbs):

> *know, understand, believe, like, love, hate, want, need, seem, own, have (possession)*

❌ *She was knowing the answer.* → ✅ *She knew the answer.*
❌ *I was wanting coffee.* → ✅ *I wanted coffee.*
