## Slide 3: "Will" for Predictions 🔮
### Talking About What We Believe Will Happen

Use **will** when you are confident about a future event — based on knowledge, logic, or belief.

**Structure:** Subject + will + base verb

| Example | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *The population will grow significantly.* | previsão baseada em dados |
| *I think he will get the job.* | previsão baseada em opinião |
| *Technology will change everything.* | afirmação com confiança |
| *It will be cold tomorrow.* | previsão geral |
| *She won't come to the party.* | previsão negativa |

> 💡 **Tip:** "Will" is for predictions you feel fairly confident about — not for scheduled events or decisions made now.

## Slide 4: "Might", "May" & "Could" for Possibilities 🌤️
### When You're Not 100% Sure

Use **might**, **may**, and **could** to show that something is possible — but not certain.

**Structure:** Subject + might / may / could + base verb

| Modal | Level of certainty | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **may** | ~50% — possibilidade real | *It may rain this afternoon.* |
| **might** | ~40% — menos certo | *I might go to the gym later.* |
| **could** | possibilidade aberta / hipotética | *That could be the answer.* |

**Negative forms:**
- *It might not work.* → talvez não funcione
- *She may not come.* → talvez ela não venha
- *He couldn't be right.* → ele não pode estar certo (diferente de "could not = impossibility")

> 💡 **May** and **might** are interchangeable in most everyday situations.  
> **Could** often implies something hypothetical or open to speculation.

## Slide 5: Probability Adverbs 📊
### How Sure Are You? Express It with Words

Probability adverbs tell your listener *how likely* something is.  
They usually come **before the main verb** or **after the verb "to be"**.

| Adverb | Certainty Level | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **definitely** | ~100% — certamente | *We will definitely need more time.* |
| **certainly** | ~95% — com certeza | *This will certainly be a challenge.* |
| **probably** | ~75% — provavelmente | *She will probably pass the exam.* |
| **perhaps / maybe** | ~50% — talvez | *Perhaps the meeting will be rescheduled.* |
| **possibly** | ~30% — possivelmente | *It could possibly work out.* |
| **unlikely** | ~10% — improvável | *It's unlikely that prices will drop.* |

**Position in sentences:**

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| Subject + **adverb** + will + verb | *She **probably** will arrive tomorrow.* |
| Subject + will + **probably** + verb | *She will **probably** arrive tomorrow.* ✅ more common |
| It + **is** + adverb + **that** + clause | *It **is likely that** they will change the plan.* |

## Slide 6: Expressing Likelihood with Adjectives 🎯
### "It is likely / unlikely / possible / probable that..."

These structures are common in formal and written English — and very useful for making clear predictions.

| Structure | Example |
|---|---|
| **It is likely that...** | *It is likely that the price will increase next year.* |
| **It is unlikely that...** | *It is unlikely that they will cancel the event.* |
| **It is possible that...** | *It is possible that she will change her mind.* |
| **It is probable that...** | *It is probable that the team will win.* |
| **There is a chance that...** | *There is a chance that flights will be delayed.* |
| **There is no chance that...** | *There is no chance that the project finishes today.* |

**Adjective + noun form:**
- *a **likely** outcome* → um resultado provável
- *an **unlikely** scenario* → um cenário improvável
- *a **possible** solution* → uma solução possível

## Slide 7: "Will" vs "Might/May" — The Difference 🆚
### Confidence Makes All the Difference

| Situation | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| You are confident — based on evidence | **will** | *The sun will set at 6 PM.* |
| You believe it's true, but aren't 100% sure | **will probably** | *She will probably be late.* |
| You think it's possible but uncertain | **might / may** | *It might rain later.* |
| It's a remote or open possibility | **could** | *We could try a different approach.* |

**Contrast:**
- *It **will** rain tomorrow.* → confiante (previsão do tempo confirma)
- *It **might** rain tomorrow.* → possível, mas incerto
- *It **could** rain, but I doubt it.* → remoto, duvidoso

## Slide 8: Common Mistakes 🚫
### Errors Brazilian Students Often Make

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Why? |
|---|---|---|
| *She will can study later.* | *She will be able to study later.* | "Can" and "will" don't combine |
| *It is probably going rain.* | *It is probably going to rain.* | Missing "to" in "going to" |
| *Maybe she wills come.* | *Maybe she will come.* | "Will" doesn't inflect |
| *It might to work.* | *It might work.* | No infinitive "to" after modals |
| *I am thinking it will rain.* | *I think it will rain.* | "Think" for opinions is not continuous |
| *Probably she is coming.* | *She will probably come.* | Adverb position in English |
| *She may be not right.* | *She may not be right.* | Negation: modal + **not** + verb |

## Slide 9: Putting It All Together 🌱
### A Natural Way to Make Predictions

**Use this structure to talk about the future fluently:**

1. **Make a prediction**
   *I think technology will change the job market.*

2. **Express the level of certainty**
   *It is likely that many jobs will disappear.*

3. **Add a possibility**
   *However, new jobs might appear as well.*

4. **Support with a reason or example**
   *For example, AI could replace routine tasks, but creative roles will probably grow.*

---

**Full example:**

> *In my opinion, the future of work will look very different. It is likely that more people will work remotely, and companies might reduce their office spaces. However, some professions — like teachers and doctors — will probably always need a human touch. There is even a chance that new careers we can't imagine today will definitely exist by then.*
