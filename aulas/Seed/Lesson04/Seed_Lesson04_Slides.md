## SLIDE 6: GRAMMAR — SIMPLE PRESENT TENSE
### Simple Present: Talking About Routines

**Visual:** Structure box with clear examples

**Structure:**
```
✅ I / You / We / They  +  BASE VERB
        I wake up at 7 AM.
        You study every day.
        We eat breakfast together.
        They sleep at 10 PM.

✅ He / She / It  +  BASE VERB + S
        He wake S up at 7 AM.
        She work S every day.
        It start S at 8 AM.
```

**Teacher Explains:**
- "When we talk about HE, SHE, or IT — we add -S to the verb!"
- Write on board: I wake up → He wake**s** up
- Ask students: "What do I add for HE/SHE?"

---

## SLIDE 7: GRAMMAR — THE -S/-ES/-IES RULE
### Adding -S to Verbs: The Rules

**Visual:** Three columns with rule + examples

| Rule | Verb | He/She/It |
|------|------|-----------|
| Most verbs: add **-S** | work | work**s** |
| | eat | eat**s** |
| | sleep | sleep**s** |
| Ends in -ch, -sh, -s, -x, -o: add **-ES** | watch | watch**es** |
| | brush | brush**es** |
| | go | go**es** |
| Ends in consonant + Y: change to **-IES** | study | stud**ies** |
| | try | tr**ies** |
| **Irregular** ⚠️ | have | **has** |

**Practice Together:**
- "I work." → "She ___?" (works)
- "I study." → "He ___?" (studies)
- "I go." → "She ___?" (goes)
- "I have." → "He ___?" (has)

## SLIDE 8: GRAMMAR — PRACTICE THE -S RULE
### Transform the Sentences!

**Visual:** Two speech bubbles (I → He/She)

**Guided Practice:**

1. "I wake up at 7 AM." → "She ___ up at 7 AM." (wakes)
2. "I study at night." → "He ___ at night." (studies)
3. "I watch TV." → "She ___ TV." (watches)
4. "I eat breakfast." → "He ___ breakfast." (eats)
5. "I have lunch at noon." → "She ___ lunch at noon." (has)
6. "I go to bed at 10 PM." → "He ___ to bed at 10 PM." (goes)

**Student Activity:**
- Each student describes a family member's routine:
  - "My mother wakes up at 6 AM. She works at a hospital."
  - "My father studies English. He goes to bed at 11 PM."

## SLIDE 9: GRAMMAR — NEGATIVE FORM
### How to Say What We DON'T Do

**Visual:** Red X with correct structure

**Structure:**
```
I / You / We / They  +  DON'T  +  BASE VERB
     I  DON'T  wake up early.
     We DON'T  work on Sundays.
     They DON'T eat breakfast.

He / She / It  +  DOESN'T  +  BASE VERB
     He   DOESN'T  wake up early.
     She  DOESN'T  study at night.
```

**Important! ⚠️**
```
✅ He doesn't WORK.     (base verb — no -S!)
❌ He doesn't WORKS.    (WRONG!)
```

**Quick Practice:**
- "I work on Sundays." → Negative: "I ___ work on Sundays." (don't)
- "She studies in the morning." → Negative: "She ___ study in the morning." (doesn't)

---

## SLIDE 10: GRAMMAR — QUESTIONS
### How to Ask About Routines

**Visual:** Question mark with structure box

**Structure:**
```
DO    +  I / you / we / they  +  BASE VERB  +  ?
    Do you wake up early?
    Do they study English?
    Do we work tomorrow?

DOES  +  he / she / it  +  BASE VERB  +  ?
    Does he wake up early?
    Does she study at night?
```

**Short Answers:**
```
Do you work?  →  Yes, I DO.  /  No, I DON'T.
Does she eat? →  Yes, she DOES.  /  No, she DOESN'T.
```

**Teacher-Student Q&A:**
- "Do you wake up early?" → "Yes, I do!" or "No, I don't."
- "Does your mother work?" → "Yes, she does."

## SLIDE 11: TIME EXPRESSIONS — IN / AT / ON
### When Do You Do It?

**Visual:** Three boxes with IN / AT / ON

```
┌──────────────────┐  ┌──────────────────┐  ┌──────────────────┐
│        IN        │  │        AT        │  │        ON        │
│                  │  │                  │  │                  │
│  in the morning  │  │  at 7 AM         │  │  on Monday       │
│  in the afternoon│  │  at noon         │  │  on weekends     │
│  in the evening  │  │  at night        │  │  on Sundays      │
│                  │  │  at midnight     │  │  on school days  │
└──────────────────┘  └──────────────────┘  └──────────────────┘
```

**Memory Trick:**
- **IN** → part of the day (morning, afternoon, evening)
- **AT** → exact time or "night" (7 AM, noon, night)
- **ON** → days of the week / weekend

**Fill in Together:**
- "I wake up ___ 7 AM." (at)
- "I exercise ___ the morning." (in)
- "I don't work ___ Sundays." (on)

## SLIDE 12: TIME EXPRESSIONS — FREQUENCY WORDS
### How Often Do You Do It?

**Visual:** Frequency scale from 0% to 100%

```
100% ──── ALWAYS   (sempre)
 80% ──── USUALLY  (geralmente)
 50% ──── SOMETIMES (às vezes)
  0% ──── NEVER    (nunca)
```

**Position:** BEFORE the main verb!
```
I  ALWAYS    wake up at 7 AM.
He USUALLY   eats breakfast.
We SOMETIMES watch TV at night.
They NEVER   work on Sundays.
```

**Quick Survey:**
- "Do you always eat breakfast?" → "I always / sometimes / never eat breakfast."
- "Do you usually study at night?" → "I usually / never study at night."

## SLIDE 13: DIALOGUE PRACTICE
### Real Conversation About Routines

**Visual:** Two people talking with speech bubbles

**Dialogue:**

**A:** What time do you wake up?  
**B:** I wake up at 7 AM. How about you?  
**A:** I wake up at 6:30 AM. Do you eat breakfast?  
**B:** Yes, I always eat breakfast! I eat at 7:30 AM.  
**A:** What do you do in the afternoon?  
**B:** I study English and I go to the gym sometimes.  
**A:** That's great! What time do you sleep?  
**B:** I usually sleep at 10 PM. I never sleep late!

**Practice:**
1. **Teacher reads, students repeat** (line by line)
2. **Read in pairs** (assign roles A and B)
3. **Personalize:** Change details to match real routines
4. **Perform:** Volunteer pairs perform for class

## SLIDE 15: GAME — ROUTINE CHAIN
### The Routine Chain Challenge!

**Visual:** Students in a circle with speech bubbles

**How to Play:**

1. First student says one routine sentence:
   - "I wake up at 7 AM."

2. Next student **repeats the previous sentence AND adds one:**
   - "She wakes up at 7 AM. I eat breakfast at 8 AM."

3. Continue around the circle — each person repeats ALL previous sentences and adds one!

4. If someone forgets or makes a mistake — start again!

**Language Focus:**
- Practice repeating with he/she (-S rule!)
- Practice sequence: "first, then, after that, finally"
- Practice memory + listening

**Variation (fewer students):**
- Teacher and student take turns building the chain together

## SLIDE 17: COMMON ERRORS — LET'S FIX THEM!
### Common Mistakes & How to Correct Them

**Visual:** Two columns — Wrong vs. Right

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct | 💡 Why? |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| He wake up at 7 AM | He wakeS up at 7 AM | He/She → add -S |
| She study every day | She studIES every day | Consonant + Y → -IES |
| He don't work | He DOESN'T work | He/She → use doesn't |
| She doesn't works | She doesn't WORK | After doesn't → base verb |
| I work IN 8 AM | I work AT 8 AM | Exact time → AT |
| I wake up IN Monday | I wake up ON Monday | Days of the week → ON |
| Does they study? | DO they study? | They → use DO |

**Activity:**
- Read each wrong sentence aloud together
- Students identify the error and correct it
- Write the correct version
