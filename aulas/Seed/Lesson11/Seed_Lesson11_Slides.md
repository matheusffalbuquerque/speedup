## SLIDE 5: SAYING WHAT HURTS
### "My ___ hurts"

Use **My ___ hurts** to say a part of your body is in pain.

**Pattern:**
```text
My + body part + hurts.
```

**Examples:**
- My head hurts.
- My back hurts.
- My stomach hurts.
- My foot hurts.
- My arm hurts.

**For plural body parts, use "hurt":**
```text
My + body parts (plural) + hurt.
```

- My eyes hurt.
- My legs hurt.
- My feet hurt.
- My shoulders hurt.

> 💡 **Remember:** Use **hurts** for one part → use **hurt** for two or more parts.

---

## SLIDE 6: SAYING WHAT YOU HAVE
### "I have a..."

Use **I have a...** to talk about an illness or a problem.

**Pattern:**
```text
I have a + problem / illness.
```

**Examples:**
- I have a headache.
- I have a cold.
- I have a fever.
- I have a stomachache.
- I have a toothache.
- I have a sore throat.

**Quick vocabulary:**

| English | Portuguese |
|---------|------------|
| **headache** | dor de cabeça |
| **stomachache** | dor de barriga |
| **toothache** | dor de dente |
| **backache** | dor nas costas |
| **sore throat** | garganta inflamada |

> 💡 **Tip:** Many body-part pains follow the pattern: **body part + ache**  
> head → **headache** | stomach → **stomachache** | tooth → **toothache** | back → **backache**

## SLIDE 7: BODY PARTS WITH POSSESSIVES
### My, Your, His, Her...

Use a **possessive adjective** before the body part to show who it belongs to.

| Possessive | Example |
|------------|---------|
| **my** | My arm hurts. |
| **your** | Your eyes are beautiful. |
| **his** | His leg is broken. |
| **her** | Her back hurts. |
| **our** | Our hands are cold. |
| **their** | Their feet are tired. |

**More examples:**
- Her head hurts.
- His stomach hurts.
- My nose is running.
- Your hand is bleeding.

**Question form:**
```text
Does your ___ hurt?
Yes, my ___ hurts.
No, my ___ doesn't hurt.
```

## SLIDE 8: AT THE DOCTOR'S
### Talking About Your Health

**Useful phrases at the doctor's office:**

| Phrase | Translation |
|--------|-------------|
| *I don't feel well.* | Não estou me sentindo bem. |
| *I am sick.* | Estou doente. |
| *My ___ hurts.* | Meu/Minha ___ dói. |
| *I have a fever.* | Estou com febre. |
| *I have a cold.* | Estou resfriado/a. |
| *I need a doctor.* | Preciso de um médico. |
| *I need medicine.* | Preciso de remédio. |
| *I feel tired.* | Estou me sentindo cansado/a. |

**Model dialogue:**
```text
Doctor: How do you feel today?
Patient: I don't feel well. My head hurts.
Doctor: Do you have a fever?
Patient: Yes, I have a fever.
Doctor: I see. Take this medicine.
Patient: Thank you, doctor.
```

## SLIDE 9: DESCRIBING HOW YOU FEEL
### "I feel..."

Use **I feel + adjective** to describe how you are feeling.

**Pattern:**
```text
I feel + adjective.
```

| Sentence | Translation |
|----------|-------------|
| *I feel sick.* | Estou me sentindo doente. |
| *I feel tired.* | Estou me sentindo cansado/a. |
| *I feel better.* | Estou me sentindo melhor. |
| *I feel good.* | Estou me sentindo bem. |
| *I feel bad.* | Estou me sentindo mal. |
| *I don't feel well.* | Não estou me sentindo bem. |

**Asking about others:**
```text
How do you feel?
How are you feeling?
Are you OK?
```

## SLIDE 11: COMMON MISTAKES
### Watch Out

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct |
|-------------|------------|
| My foots hurt. | My **feet** hurt. |
| My tooths hurt. | My **teeth** hurt. |
| I have headache. | I have **a** headache. |
| My legs hurts. | My legs **hurt**. |
| She feel sick. | She **feels** sick. |
| I have a cold in my head. | I have a **headache**. |
| My stomach is hurting me. | My stomach **hurts**. |
| He is sick on the hospital. | He is sick **in** the hospital. |

**Final model sentences:**
```text
I don't feel well.
My head and stomach hurt.
I have a fever.
I need a doctor.
```
