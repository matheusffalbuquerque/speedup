## SLIDE 4: "I LIKE TO..." — Talking About Hobbies
### Main Structure #1

**Pattern:**
```text
I like to + verb (base form).
```

**Examples:**
- I like to **read**.
- I like to **play** football.
- I like to **watch** series.
- I like to **listen** to music.

**Ask a friend:**
```text
What do you like to do?
I like to dance.
```

---

## SLIDE 5: "I ENJOY..." — Another Way to Say It
### Main Structure #2

**Pattern:**
```text
I enjoy + verb-ing.
```

**Examples:**
- I enjoy **reading**.
- I enjoy **playing** games.
- I enjoy **watching** movies.
- I enjoy **cooking** at home.

**Both structures mean the same thing:**

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| I like **to read**. | ✅ correct |
| I enjoy **reading**. | ✅ correct |

---

## SLIDE 6: VERB + -ING
### How to Form It

Add **-ing** to the base verb.

| Base Verb | + -ing | Note |
|-----------|--------|------|
| play | play**ing** | just add -ing |
| watch | watch**ing** | just add -ing |
| listen | listen**ing** | just add -ing |
| read | read**ing** | just add -ing |
| dance | danc**ing** | drop the final **e** |
| run | runn**ing** | double the final consonant |
| swim | swimm**ing** | double the final consonant |

**Quick rule:**
```text
verb ending in -e  →  drop e + -ing   (dance → dancing)
short verb + consonant  →  double it   (run → running)
all others  →  just add -ing
```

## SLIDE 7: FREQUENCY ADVERBS
### How Often Do You Do It?

```text
ALWAYS → USUALLY → SOMETIMES → RARELY → NEVER
100%       75%        50%          25%       0%
```

| Adverb | Portuguese | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **always** | sempre | I always listen to music. |
| **usually** | geralmente | I usually watch series at night. |
| **sometimes** | às vezes | I sometimes play football. |
| **rarely** | raramente | I rarely cook dinner. |
| **never** | nunca | I never watch horror movies. |

**Where to put it:**
```text
I + adverb + verb.
I always read before bed.
I never play video games at work.
```

---

## SLIDE 8: COMBINING IDEAS
### Frequency + Hobby

**Pattern:**
```text
I + frequency adverb + like to / enjoy + verb(-ing).
```

**Examples:**
- I **always** enjoy **reading** before bed.
- I **sometimes** like to **cook** on weekends.
- I **usually** watch a movie on Fridays.
- I **never** play football — I prefer swimming.

**Talking about preferences:**
```text
I like... but I prefer...
I enjoy... because...
```

- I like watching movies, but I **prefer** reading.
- I enjoy dancing **because** it's fun.

---

## SLIDE 9: COMMON MISTAKES
### Watch Out

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct |
|-------------|------------|
| I like play football. | I like **to play** football. |
| I enjoy to read. | I enjoy **reading**. |
| I am never watch TV. | I **never** watch TV. |
| My hobby are games. | My hobby **is** games. |
| I like the music. | I like music. |
| I always am tired. | I am always tired. |
| She enjoy cooking. | She enjoy**s** cooking. |

**Final model:**
```text
My name is Ana. In my free time, I enjoy watching series and listening to music.
I sometimes play volleyball on weekends. I never read — but I want to start!
```
