## SLIDE 4: REVIEW — VERB "TO BE"
### Am, Is, Are

Use the verb **to be** to talk about identity, age, feelings, colors, weather, and descriptions.

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **I** | am | I am Brazilian. |
| **You** | are | You are my friend. |
| **He** | is | He is a doctor. |
| **She** | is | She is happy. |
| **It** | is | It is cold today. |
| **We** | are | We are students. |
| **They** | are | They are at home. |

**Negative form:**
```text
I am not tired.
She is not sick.
They are not expensive.
```

**Short forms:**
- I am = I'm
- He is = He's
- She is = She's
- They are = They're

## SLIDE 6: REVIEW — SIMPLE PRESENT
### Talking About Routines and Preferences

Use the **Simple Present** for routines, habits, likes, and general facts.

**With I / you / we / they:**
```text
I work in the morning.
They study English.
We like music.
```

**With he / she / it, add -s:**
```text
He works in the morning.
She studies English.
It costs ten dollars.
```

**Negative form:**
```text
I don't like coffee.
She doesn't work on Sunday.
They don't play soccer.
```

**Common verbs from Seed:**
> work · study · sleep · like · play · watch · listen · read · buy · cost · feel

**Places in the city:**

## SLIDE 9: REVIEW — BASIC QUESTIONS
### Asking for Information

Questions help you start conversations and get information.

| Question | Use | Example Answer |
|----------|-----|----------------|
| **What is your name?** | name | My name is Julia. |
| **How are you?** | feelings | I am fine, thanks. |
| **How old are you?** | age | I am eighteen years old. |
| **Do you like...?** | preference | Yes, I do. / No, I don't. |
| **Where is...?** | location | It's near the park. |
| **What's the weather like?** | weather | It's sunny. |
| **How much is it?** | price | It's ten dollars. |
| **How do you feel?** | health | I feel sick. |

**Question word review:**
> what = o que / qual  
> where = onde  
> how = como  
> how much = quanto custa  
> how old = quantos anos

## SLIDE 11: "I WANT TO..." FOR GOALS
### Saying What You Want

Use **I want to + verb** to talk about wishes, plans, and personal goals.

**Pattern:**
```text
I want to + base verb.
```

**Examples:**
- I want to learn English.
- I want to speak better.
- I want to travel.
- I want to study every day.
- I want to understand movies in English.
- I want to work with international people.

**Other subjects:**
```text
He wants to + verb.
She wants to + verb.
They want to + verb.
```

Examples:
- She wants to learn English.
- He wants to travel.
- They want to practice more.

> 💡 Remember: with **he** and **she**, use **wants**.

---

## SLIDE 12: "I WILL..." FOR SIMPLE FUTURE
### Introduction to Future Sentences

Use **will** to talk about future decisions, promises, and goals.

**Pattern:**
```text
Subject + will + base verb.
```

**Examples:**
- I will study English.
- I will practice every day.
- I will learn new words.
- I will speak with confidence.
- I will travel in the future.

**Negative form:**
```text
Subject + will not + base verb.
```

Short form:
```text
will not = won't
```

Examples:
- I won't give up.
- I won't stop learning.
- She won't study at night.

> 💡 After **will**, use the base verb.  
> ✅ I will study.  
> ❌ I will studies.

---

## SLIDE 13: PRESENT + FUTURE
### Talking About Now and Next

You can combine what you do now with what you want for the future.

**Pattern 1:**
```text
Now, I + simple present.
In the future, I will + verb.
```

**Examples:**
- Now, I study English. In the future, I will travel.
- Now, I work in Brazil. In the future, I will work with English.
- Now, I watch movies with subtitles. In the future, I will understand movies in English.

**Pattern 2:**
```text
I like + noun / verb-ing.
I want to + verb.
```

**Examples:**
- I like music. I want to understand English songs.
- I enjoy playing soccer. I want to talk about sports in English.
- I like meeting people. I want to speak English with new friends.
