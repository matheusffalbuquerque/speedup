## SLIDE 9: STRUCTURE 1 - PREPOSITIONS OF PLACE
### Where Is It? - Preposições de Lugar

**Visual:** City map showing different position relationships between buildings

**The Prepositions:**

| English | Português | Use |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| **IN** | em / dentro de | inside an area or city |
| **ON** | em / na | on a street, surface |
| **AT** | em / no | at a specific location |
| **NEXT TO** | ao lado de | directly beside |
| **NEAR** | perto de | close to, not far |

**Visual Examples on Map:**
- The pharmacy is **next to** the hospital.
- The park is **near** the school.
- The bank is **on** Main Street.
- The restaurant is **in** the city center.
- She is **at** the supermarket.

## SLIDE 10: STRUCTURE 1 - IN / ON / AT (Deep Dive)
### When to Use IN, ON, or AT?

**Visual:** Three columns with examples and icons

**📍 IN** — inside, within an area
```
in the park        (dentro do parque)
in the city        (na cidade)
in the hospital    (no hospital)
in the school      (na escola)
```

**📍 ON** — on a surface, on a street
```
on the street      (na rua)
on Main Street     (na Rua Principal)
on the corner      (na esquina)
```

**📍 AT** — at a specific place/address
```
at the bank        (no banco)
at the supermarket (no supermercado)
at the restaurant  (no restaurante)
at school          (na escola — no article!)
```

**🔑 Special Rule:**
- "at school" / "at home" / "at work" → NO article "the"
- "at the school" / "at the bank" → WITH article "the" (specific place)

---

## SLIDE 11: STRUCTURE 1 - NEXT TO / NEAR
### Side by Side vs. Close By

**Visual:** City block diagram showing two buildings next to each other and two buildings that are near each other (but not adjacent)

**📍 NEXT TO** — directly beside, touching or very close
```
The pharmacy is next to the hospital.
The bank is next to the restaurant.
The supermarket is next to the park.
```
→ *Eles compartilham uma parede ou são vizinhos imediatos*

**📍 NEAR** — close to, but not directly beside
```
The school is near the park.
The hospital is near the bank.
My house is near the supermarket.
```
→ *Estão próximos, mas podem ter outras coisas entre eles*

**Key Difference:**
- **Next to** = exatamente ao lado (walls touching)
- **Near** = nas proximidades (a few buildings or blocks away)

## SLIDE 12: STRUCTURE 2 - "WHERE IS...?"
### Asking About Location

**Visual:** Person looking at a map with a question mark

**Question Structure:**
```
WHERE + IS + place?

WHERE IS the school?
WHERE IS the hospital?
WHERE IS the bank?
WHERE IS the park?
```

**Answer Structures:**
```
It's IN + [area/city]
It's ON + [street name]
It's AT + [specific address]
It's NEXT TO + [building]
It's NEAR + [building/area]
```

**Examples:**
- **Where is the school?** → It's on Park Street.
- **Where is the hospital?** → It's near the park.
- **Where is the pharmacy?** → It's next to the hospital.
- **Where is the restaurant?** → It's in the city center.

## SLIDE 14: STRUCTURE 3 - "THERE IS / THERE ARE"
### Talking About What Exists

**Visual:** City scene with arrows pointing to various places

**Introducing:**
Use THERE IS / THERE ARE to say something EXISTS in a place

**Structure:**
```
THERE IS  + singular noun
THERE ARE + plural noun
```

**Affirmative:**
```
There IS  a school on this street.
There ARE two parks in the city.
There IS  a hospital near my house.
There ARE many restaurants downtown.
```

**Negative:**
```
There IS NOT (There ISN'T) a bank here.
There ARE NOT (There AREN'T) any pharmacies near here.
```

**Question:**
```
IS THERE a school near here?   → Yes, there is. / No, there isn't.
ARE THERE any restaurants?     → Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.
```

---

## SLIDE 15: STRUCTURE 3 - THERE IS vs. THERE ARE
### Singular or Plural?

**Visual:** Table comparing "There is" and "There are" with examples and images

**THERE IS** → 1 item (singular)

```
There is a school.        (Uma escola)
There is a park.          (Um parque)
There is a pharmacy.      (Uma farmácia)
There is a hospital.      (Um hospital)
```

**THERE ARE** → 2+ items (plural)

```
There are two schools.       (Duas escolas)
There are many parks.        (Muitos parques)
There are three restaurants. (Três restaurantes)
There are some banks.        (Alguns bancos)
```

**⚠️ Common Mistake:**
- ❌ "There are a school."
- ✅ "There is a school."
- ❌ "There is two banks."
- ✅ "There are two banks."

## SLIDE 20: COMMON ERRORS - LET'S FIX THEM!
### Mistakes to Avoid

**Visual:** Wrong vs. Right comparisons with city icons

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| The school is **in** Oak Street | The school is **on** Oak Street |
| There is **two** hospitals | There **are** two hospitals |
| Where **are** the pharmacy? | Where **is** the pharmacy? |
| It's **next** the bank | It's **next to** the bank |
| There is **a** pharmacies | There **are** pharmacies |
| The park is **to near** the school | The park is **near** the school |
| Is **there are** a hospital? | Is **there** a hospital? |
| The restaurant is **on** downtown | The restaurant is **in** downtown |

**Why These Mistakes Happen:**
- IN vs. ON: In Portuguese, both can be "em" → check if it's an area (in) or street (on)
- IS vs. ARE: Match the verb to the noun (singular = is, plural = are)
- NEXT TO: It's always TWO words — don't forget "to"!

## SLIDE 21: CULTURAL NOTE
### Cities in English-Speaking Countries

**Visual:** Split image showing a typical US/UK street vs. a Brazilian street

**🇺🇸 🇬🇧 City Culture:**

**Street Naming:**
- Streets have names: "Main Street", "Oak Avenue", "Park Boulevard"
- Addresses include number + street: "42 Oak Street"
- Blocks are used for distance: "It's two blocks away"

**Common City Places:**
- **Drugstore** = another word for pharmacy (USA)
- **Chemist's** = pharmacy (UK)
- **Grocery store** = smaller supermarket (USA)
- **Off-licence** = liquor store (UK)

**Asking for Directions:**
- Always say "Excuse me" first — very polite!
- "Is it far?" is a common question
- "You can't miss it!" = você não tem como errar!

**🇧🇷 Comparison with Brazil:**
- In Brazil, "farmácia" and "drogaria" are slightly different
- In the USA, "pharmacy" and "drugstore" are often the same place
- Both cultures use similar places — the vocabulary just changes!

## SLIDE 23: READING - A DAY IN THE CITY
### Read and Understand

**Visual:** Illustrated story panels showing a person visiting different places

**Short Text:**

*"My name is Laura. I live in a big city. In my neighborhood, there is a school, a park, and a supermarket.*

*Every morning, I walk my children to school. The school is on Park Street, near the park. After that, I go to the supermarket. The supermarket is next to the bank, on Main Street.*

*On Tuesdays, I go to the pharmacy. The pharmacy is next to the hospital. It's near my house.*

*On weekends, my family goes to the park. There are two restaurants near the park. We love the Italian restaurant — it's next to the bank!*

*I love my neighborhood. There are many useful places near my home!"*

**Comprehension Questions (Teacher Asks Orally):**
1. "Where is the school?" → On Park Street, near the park.
2. "What is next to the bank?" → The supermarket (and the restaurant).
3. "Where is the pharmacy?" → Next to the hospital.
4. "Is there a restaurant near the park?" → Yes, there are two.
5. "Does Laura like her neighborhood?" → Yes, she loves it!
