Ao final desta lição, os alunos serão capazes de:
1. Identificar e nomear os principais lugares de uma cidade em inglês
2. Usar preposições de lugar (in, on, at, next to, near, between, across from, behind, in front of)
3. Perguntar e dizer onde algo está: "Where is...?" / "It's in/on/at..."
4. Usar "There is" e "There are" para descrever o que existe em uma cidade
5. Compreender e produzir diálogos simples pedindo e dando direções
6. Descrever a localização de lugares em relação a outros

## 🔧 ESTRUTURAS LINGUÍSTICAS

### 1. Prepositions of Place (Preposições de Lugar)

Preposições de lugar indicam **onde** algo ou alguém está. Em inglês, elas são posicionadas antes do lugar de referência.

**Estrutura:** Subject + verb + **preposition** + place

---

#### IN — dentro de / em (espaço fechado ou delimitado)

Usamos **IN** quando algo está dentro de um espaço com limites definidos: uma cidade, um bairro, um edifício.

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Inside a building | The doctor **is in** the hospital. |
| Inside a city | The bank **is in** downtown. |
| Inside a neighborhood | There is a park **in** my neighborhood. |
| General enclosed area | The children **are in** school. |

**More Examples:**
- The pharmacy **is in** the shopping mall.
- I study **in** a school near my house.
- She works **in** a restaurant **in** the city.
- The library **is in** the center of the city.

> 💡 **Dica:** Use IN para lugares grandes ou fechados — uma cidade, um bairro, um edifício.

---

#### ON — em (superfície ou rua/avenida)

Usamos **ON** quando algo está sobre uma superfície ou **em uma rua específica**.

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| On a street/avenue | The bank **is on** Main Street. |
| On a corner | The pharmacy **is on** the corner. |
| On a surface | The map **is on** the table. |

**More Examples:**
- The supermarket **is on** Oak Avenue.
- There is a restaurant **on** this street.
- The bus stop **is on** Second Street.
- The school **is on** Park Avenue.

> 💡 **Dica:** Use ON para ruas e avenidas — "on + nome da rua/avenida".

---

#### AT — em (lugar específico / endereço)

Usamos **AT** para indicar um ponto específico, um endereço ou um lugar exato.

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Specific address | The school **is at** 15 Oak Street. |
| Specific point/location | Meet me **at** the bus stop. |
| Common fixed expressions | **at** home / **at** work / **at** school |

**More Examples:**
- She **is at** the hospital right now.
- I'll meet you **at** the park entrance.
- He's **at** the supermarket.
- The meeting **is at** the bank.

> 💡 **Dica:** Use AT para pontos específicos — um endereço, um lugar exato, um ponto de encontro.

---

#### NEXT TO — ao lado de

Indica que algo está imediatamente ao lado de outro lugar.

**Structure:** Place A + is + **next to** + Place B

**Examples:**
- The pharmacy **is next to** the supermarket.
- The bank **is next to** the restaurant.
- My school **is next to** a park.
- The bus stop **is next to** the hotel.

---

#### NEAR — perto de

Indica proximidade, mas não necessariamente ao lado imediato.

**Structure:** Place A + is + **near** + Place B

**Examples:**
- The hospital **is near** the city center.
- Is there a bank **near** here?
- The park **is near** my house.
- There is a pharmacy **near** the school.

> 💡 **Near vs. Next to:**  
> - **Next to** = exatamente ao lado (touching or right beside)  
> - **Near** = nas proximidades (close but not necessarily beside)

---

#### BETWEEN — entre (dois lugares)

Indica que algo está no meio de outros dois lugares.

**Structure:** Place A + is + **between** + Place B + **and** + Place C

**Examples:**
- The park **is between** the school **and** the hospital.
- The bakery **is between** the bank **and** the post office.
- The hotel **is between** two restaurants.

---

#### ACROSS FROM — em frente a / do outro lado de

Indica que algo está do lado oposto, geralmente de uma rua.

**Structure:** Place A + is + **across from** + Place B

**Examples:**
- The bank **is across from** the supermarket.
- The park **is across from** the school.
- The pharmacy **is across from** the hospital.

---

#### IN FRONT OF — em frente de (do mesmo lado)

Indica que algo está na parte da frente de outro lugar, do mesmo lado.

**Structure:** Place A + is + **in front of** + Place B

**Examples:**
- The bus stop **is in front of** the hospital.
- There is a garden **in front of** the church.
- The taxi **is in front of** the hotel.

---

#### BEHIND — atrás de

Indica que algo está na parte de trás de outro lugar.

**Structure:** Place A + is + **behind** + Place B

**Examples:**
- The parking lot **is behind** the supermarket.
- The garden **is behind** the restaurant.
- The small park **is behind** the school.

---

### Prepositions Quick Reference Table

| Preposition | Português | Use For | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| **in** | em / dentro de | cities, neighborhoods, buildings | in the city, in the hospital |
| **on** | em / na | streets, avenues, surfaces | on Main Street, on Oak Avenue |
| **at** | em / no | specific points, addresses | at the bus stop, at 10 Park Street |
| **next to** | ao lado de | immediately beside | next to the bank |
| **near** | perto de | close by (not exact) | near the school |
| **between** | entre | between two places | between the park and the church |
| **across from** | em frente a / do outro lado | opposite side (usually a street) | across from the supermarket |
| **in front of** | em frente de | in front of (same side) | in front of the hotel |
| **behind** | atrás de | at the back of | behind the restaurant |

### 2. "Where is...?" — Perguntando sobre localização

**Function:** Perguntar onde um lugar ou coisa está localizado.

**Structure:** **Where is** + the + place + ?

---

#### Affirmative Form — Forma Afirmativa

**Pattern:** The [place] **is** + preposition + location.

| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| The hospital **is on** Main Street. | O hospital fica na Main Street. |
| The school **is near** the park. | A escola fica perto do parque. |
| The bank **is next to** the supermarket. | O banco fica ao lado do supermercado. |
| The pharmacy **is at** the corner. | A farmácia fica na esquina. |
| The restaurant **is in** downtown. | O restaurante fica no centro. |

---

#### Negative Form — Forma Negativa

**Pattern:** The [place] **is not / isn't** + preposition + location.

| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| The bank **isn't on** this street. | O banco não fica nessa rua. |
| The hospital **is not near** here. | O hospital não fica perto daqui. |
| The park **isn't behind** the school. | O parque não fica atrás da escola. |

---

#### Interrogative Form — Forma Interrogativa

**Pattern:** **Where is** the [place]? / **Is** the [place] + preposition + location?

| Question | Short Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **Where is** the hospital? | It's **on** Main Street. |
| **Where is** the school? | It's **near** the park. |
| **Where is** the bank? | It's **next to** the supermarket. |
| **Is** the pharmacy **near** here? | Yes, it is. / No, it isn't. |
| **Is** the park **behind** the school? | Yes, it is. / No, it isn't. |

**Common Question + Answer Patterns:**
```
Q: Where is the supermarket?
A: It's on Oak Avenue, next to the bank.

Q: Is there a pharmacy near here?
A: Yes, there is. It's across from the hospital.

Q: Where is the bus stop?
A: It's in front of the hotel, on Second Street.
```

---

### 3. "There is / There are" — Indicando o que existe

**Function:** Informar que algo existe (ou não existe) em um lugar.

- Use **THERE IS** → com substantivos **singulares** ou **incontáveis**
- Use **THERE ARE** → com substantivos **plurais**

---

#### Affirmative Form — Forma Afirmativa

**Pattern:** **There is** + a/an + singular noun + location  
**Pattern:** **There are** + number/some + plural noun + location

| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| **There is** a hospital on Main Street. | Há um hospital na Main Street. |
| **There is** a park near my school. | Há um parque perto da minha escola. |
| **There is** a pharmacy next to the supermarket. | Há uma farmácia ao lado do supermercado. |
| **There are** two banks in the city center. | Há dois bancos no centro da cidade. |
| **There are** many restaurants on Oak Avenue. | Há muitos restaurantes na Oak Avenue. |
| **There are** some bus stops near the hotel. | Há alguns pontos de ônibus perto do hotel. |

**Contractions (Common in Speaking):**
- There is → **There's**
- There are → (no common contraction in formal speech)

**With Contractions:**
- **There's** a supermarket on this street.
- **There's** a park behind the school.

---

#### Negative Form — Forma Negativa

**Pattern:** **There is no** + noun / **There isn't a** + noun + location  
**Pattern:** **There are no** + noun / **There aren't any** + noun + location

| Example | Translation |
|---------|-------------|
| **There isn't a** hospital near here. | Não há hospital perto daqui. |
| **There is no** bank on this street. | Não há banco nessa rua. |
| **There aren't any** restaurants in this neighborhood. | Não há restaurantes nesse bairro. |
| **There are no** parks near the city center. | Não há parques perto do centro. |

> 💡 **IS NO vs. ISN'T A:**  
> - **There is no** bank = **There isn't a** bank (same meaning)  
> - **There are no** restaurants = **There aren't any** restaurants (same meaning)

---

#### Interrogative Form — Forma Interrogativa

**Pattern:** **Is there** a/an + singular noun + location?  
**Pattern:** **Are there** any + plural noun + location?

| Question | Short Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| **Is there** a hospital near here? | Yes, there is. / No, there isn't. |
| **Is there** a supermarket on this street? | Yes, there is. / No, there isn't. |
| **Are there** any restaurants downtown? | Yes, there are. / No, there aren't. |
| **Are there** any parks in this neighborhood? | Yes, there are. / No, there aren't. |
| **How many** banks **are there** in the city? | There are three banks. |

---

#### "There is / There are" — Complete Overview

| Form | Singular | Plural |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Affirmative** | There **is** a park near here. | There **are** two banks downtown. |
| **Negative** | There **isn't** a hospital on this street. | There **aren't** any restaurants here. |
| **Question** | **Is there** a pharmacy? | **Are there** any schools? |
| **Short Answer (+)** | Yes, there **is**. | Yes, there **are**. |
| **Short Answer (−)** | No, there **isn't**. | No, there **aren't**. |

## 🎮 ATIVIDADE DE AQUECIMENTO (WARM-UP)

### Map Drawing Challenge — Desenhando o Mapa da Vizinhança

**Duração:** 10 minutos  
**Objetivo:** Ativar vocabulário de lugares, estimular o pensamento espacial e preparar o aluno para usar preposições de lugar  
**Formato:** Atividade individual

---

**Materiais:**
- Uma folha de papel em branco (ou caderno)
- Lápis ou caneta

---

**Procedimento:**

**Phase 1: Teacher Introduction (2 min)**  
1. O professor desenha no quadro um mapa simples com algumas ruas e lugares:
   - Uma rua principal horizontal
   - Uma rua secundária vertical cruzando
   - 3-4 lugares simples: a school, a park, a supermarket, a bank
2. Enquanto desenha, o professor fala em inglês:
   - *"Here is Main Street. On Main Street, there is a school."*
   - *"Next to the school, there is a park."*
   - *"Across from the park, there is a bank."*

**Phase 2: Individual Drawing (6 min)**  
1. O professor instrui o aluno a desenhar o mapa de sua própria vizinhança (ou imaginar uma cidade):
   - *"Now, draw your neighborhood! Draw streets and places."*
2. O aluno deve incluir pelo menos **5 lugares** do vocabulário da lição
3. O aluno escreve os nomes dos lugares no mapa em inglês

**Phase 3: Description (2 min)**  
1. O aluno descreve seu mapa em voz alta usando frases simples:
   - *"There is a school on my street."*
   - *"The supermarket is next to the park."*
   - *"There is a pharmacy near my house."*

---

**Language the Student Should Use:**
- "There is a ___ on ___ Street."
- "The ___ is next to the ___."
- "There is a ___ near my house."

---

> 🗒️ **Nota para o Professor:** Se o aluno tiver dificuldade em lembrar os nomes dos lugares, deixe o vocabulário visível no quadro ou em uma lista. O objetivo do warm-up é **ativar**, não testar.

## 📖 CONTEÚDO DA LIÇÃO (LESSON CONTENT)

### PART 1: Places in the City — Vocabulary (10 min)

**Step 1: Core Vocabulary Introduction (4 min)**
- Apresentar os 8 lugares principais com imagens ou desenhos no quadro
- Dizer cada palavra claramente, o aluno repete 3x
- Associar cada lugar a uma função:
  - *"A HOSPITAL — you go there when you're sick."*
  - *"A BANK — you go there to get money."*
  - *"A PHARMACY — you go there to buy medicine."*
  - *"A SUPERMARKET — you go there to buy food."*
  - *"A SCHOOL — you go there to study."*
  - *"A RESTAURANT — you go there to eat."*
  - *"A PARK — you go there to relax."*
  - *"A STREET — it connects all these places."*

**Step 2: What Do You Find There? (3 min)**
- O professor pergunta, o aluno responde:
  - *"Where do you go to eat dinner?"* → **A restaurant!**
  - *"Where do you go to buy aspirin?"* → **A pharmacy!**
  - *"Where do you go to study?"* → **A school!**
  - *"Where do you go to save money?"* → **A bank!**

**Step 3: Expand with Extra Vocabulary (3 min)**
- Introduzir 4-5 lugares extras (library, bakery, post office, bus stop, hotel)
- Mostrar imagem ou desenho para cada
- Aluno repete e associa à função

---

### PART 2: Prepositions of Place (15 min)

**Step 1: Visual Introduction with Map (5 min)**
- Desenhar um mapa simples no quadro com ruas e lugares
- Usar o mapa para demonstrar cada preposição:
  - *"The school is **on** Park Street."*
  - *"The bank is **next to** the school."*
  - *"The park is **across from** the bank."*
  - *"The pharmacy is **between** the supermarket and the hospital."*

**Step 2: Preposition Drills (5 min)**
- Professor aponta para o mapa e pergunta, aluno responde usando a preposição correta:
  - *"Where is the school?"* → **"It's on Park Street."**
  - *"Is the bank next to the school?"* → **"Yes, it is."**
  - *"Is the park behind the hospital?"* → **"No, it isn't. It's across from the bank."**

**Step 3: Contrast and Clarify (5 min)**
- Comparar as preposições que causam mais confusão:
  - **IN** (within) vs. **ON** (on a surface/street) vs. **AT** (at a point)
    - *"I am **in** the city."* (dentro da cidade)
    - *"The bank **is on** Oak Street."* (na rua)
    - *"I am **at** the bank."* (no banco — ponto exato)
  - **NEAR** vs. **NEXT TO**
    - *"The pharmacy **is near** the hospital."* (perto, mas não necessariamente ao lado)
    - *"The pharmacy **is next to** the hospital."* (exatamente ao lado)

---

### PART 3: "Where is...?" Structure (10 min)

**Step 1: Question Formation (4 min)**
- Escrever no quadro:
  ```
  Where + is + the + place + ?
  Where is the hospital?
  Where is the school?
  Where is the nearest bank?
  ```
- Modelar a pronúncia: **WHERE IS** (spoken naturally: "where's")
- Praticar entonação: voz sobe no final da pergunta

**Step 2: Answer Patterns (3 min)**
- Mostrar os padrões de resposta:
  ```
  It's on ___ Street.
  It's next to the ___.
  It's near the ___.
  It's at the corner of ___ and ___.
  ```

**Step 3: Question and Answer Drills (3 min)**
- Professor usa o mapa no quadro para fazer perguntas, aluno responde:
  - *"Where is the supermarket?"* → **"It's on Oak Avenue, next to the bank."**
  - *"Is the park near the school?"* → **"Yes, it is. It's across from the school."**
  - *"Where is the bus stop?"* → **"It's in front of the hotel."**

---

### PART 4: "There is / There are" Structure (10 min)

**Step 1: Introduction (3 min)**
- Escrever no quadro a estrutura completa:
  ```
  THERE IS  + a/an + singular noun
  THERE ARE + number/some + plural noun
  ```
- Explicar em português: *"Usamos 'there is' e 'there are' para dizer que algo EXISTE em um lugar."*
- Comparar com o português: "Há um banco..." = "There is a bank..."

**Step 2: Classroom to City (3 min)**
- Praticar primeiro com a sala de aula (contexto imediato):
  - *"There is a board in this classroom."*
  - *"There are chairs in this room."*
  - *"There isn't a TV in this room."*
- Depois expandir para a cidade:
  - *"There is a hospital on Main Street."*
  - *"There are many restaurants downtown."*
  - *"There aren't any parks in this neighborhood."*

**Step 3: Question Practice (4 min)**
- Praticar as formas interrogativas:
  ```
  Is there a pharmacy near here?   → Yes, there is. / No, there isn't.
  Are there any restaurants nearby? → Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.
  How many banks are there?        → There are two banks.
  ```

---

### PART 5: Integration — Using Everything Together (5 min)

**Full Description of a City:**

O professor modela uma descrição completa usando todas as estruturas:

> *"This is my city. There is a school on Park Street. The school is next to a park. Across from the park, there is a supermarket. The bank is between the supermarket and a restaurant. Near the bank, there is a pharmacy. There are many restaurants downtown. The hospital is on Main Street, in front of the bus stop."*

- Destacar o uso de cada estrutura ao descrever
- Aluno tenta descrever o mapa que desenhou no warm-up usando as mesmas estruturas

## 💡 EXEMPLOS E DIÁLOGOS

### Dialogue 1: Asking for Directions — Pedindo Informações

**Tourist:** Excuse me. Is there a bank near here?  
**Local:** Yes, there is. There's a bank on Main Street.  
**Tourist:** Where is Main Street?  
**Local:** It's two blocks from here. Turn left, and the bank is next to the supermarket.  
**Tourist:** Is there a pharmacy near the bank?  
**Local:** Yes! There's a pharmacy across from the bank.  
**Tourist:** Thank you so much!  
**Local:** You're welcome!

---

> 📝 **Vocabulary Notes — Dialogue 1:**
> - *Excuse me* = Com licença (to get someone's attention)
> - *near here* = perto daqui
> - *two blocks from here* = dois quarteirões daqui
> - *Turn left* = Vire à esquerda
> - *You're welcome* = De nada

---

### Dialogue 2: Describing the Neighborhood — Descrevendo a Vizinhança

**Ana:** Do you like your neighborhood?  
**Tom:** Yes, I love it! There are many places near my house.  
**Ana:** Really? Like what?  
**Tom:** Well, there's a supermarket on Oak Street, next to my building.  
**Ana:** That's convenient! Is there a park nearby?  
**Tom:** Yes! There's a big park behind the school. I go there on weekends.  
**Ana:** What about restaurants?  
**Tom:** There are three restaurants on Main Avenue. My favorite is across from the bank.  
**Ana:** Sounds like a great neighborhood!

---

> 📝 **Vocabulary Notes — Dialogue 2:**
> - *I love it!* = Eu adoro! (stronger than "I like it")
> - *Really?* = Mesmo? / Sério?
> - *convenient* = conveniente / prático
> - *nearby* = nas proximidades / por aqui perto
> - *on weekends* = nos fins de semana
> - *What about...?* = E quanto a...? / E...?
> - *Sounds like...* = Parece... / Soa como...

---

### Dialogue 3: At the Information Desk — Na Recepção do Hotel

**Guest:** Good morning! I'm looking for a pharmacy. Where is the nearest one?  
**Receptionist:** Good morning! There is a pharmacy on Second Street, across from the post office.  
**Guest:** Is it far from here?  
**Receptionist:** No, it's very close. It's just in front of the bus stop, one block away.  
**Guest:** Is there a restaurant near the pharmacy?  
**Receptionist:** Yes! There are two restaurants on Second Street. One is next to the pharmacy, and the other is between the pharmacy and the park.  
**Guest:** Perfect! And where is the bus stop exactly?  
**Receptionist:** The bus stop is at the corner of Second Street and Oak Avenue. You can't miss it!  
**Guest:** Thank you very much!  
**Receptionist:** My pleasure. Have a great day!

---

> 📝 **Vocabulary Notes — Dialogue 3:**
> - *I'm looking for...* = Estou procurando...
> - *nearest* = o/a mais próximo(a)
> - *far from here* = longe daqui
> - *just in front of* = bem em frente de
> - *one block away* = a um quarteirão daqui
> - *at the corner of* = na esquina de... com...
> - *You can't miss it!* = Você não tem como errar! / É fácil de encontrar!
> - *My pleasure* = Com prazer / De nada (more formal than "You're welcome")

   - Durante a semana, observe os lugares ao redor de onde você mora

## 📌 COMMON ERRORS & CORRECTIONS

| ❌ Common Error | ✅ Correction | 💡 Explanation |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|
| The hospital is in Oak Street. | The hospital **is on** Oak Street. | Ruas e avenidas usam ON, não IN |
| I am in the bus stop. | I am **at** the bus stop. | Pontos específicos usam AT |
| There is a restaurants here. | There **are** restaurants here. | "Restaurants" é plural → use ARE |
| There are a park near here. | There **is** a park near here. | "A park" é singular → use IS |
| Is the park next to? | Where **is** the park? / **Is** the park next to the school? | A pergunta precisa estar completa |
| The school is near to the park. | The school is **near** the park. | "Near" não usa "to" depois |
| There is no any bank here. | There **isn't** a bank / There **is no** bank here. | Não misturar "no" e "any" |
| Where is the bank is? | **Where is** the bank? | Não repetir o verbo "is" |
| The pharmacy is between the bank. | The pharmacy is **between the bank and the school**. | "Between" sempre precisa de dois lugares |

## 🌟 DICAS CULTURAIS

### Cities in English-Speaking Countries

**1. Asking for Directions:**
- Em países como EUA, Reino Unido e Austrália, é completamente normal parar alguém na rua e pedir direções.
- É educado começar com: *"Excuse me..."* (Com licença...)
- As pessoas geralmente são prestativas e dão direções detalhadas.
- *"You can't miss it!"* é uma expressão comum para dizer que o lugar é fácil de encontrar.

**2. How Americans Give Directions:**
- Americanos frequentemente usam **blocos** (blocks) como medida:
  - *"It's three blocks down this street."* (São três quarteirões nessa rua.)
  - *"Turn left at the second block."* (Vire à esquerda no segundo quarteirão.)
- Também usam os **pontos cardeais**: *"Go north on Main Street."*

**3. How British People Give Directions:**
- Britânicos costumam usar **landmarks** (pontos de referência) para dar direções:
  - *"Go past the church and turn right."* (Passe pela igreja e vire à direita.)
  - *"It's just opposite the pub."* (Fica bem em frente ao pub.)
- *"Opposite"* = across from (mais comum no inglês britânico)

**4. City Layout Differences:**
- **American cities:** Frequentemente organizadas em **grid system** (sistema de grade) com ruas numeradas e nomeadas. Ex: *"It's on 5th Avenue and 42nd Street."*
- **British cities:** Mais orgânicas e históricas, com ruas tortuosas e nomes tradicionais.
- **Australian cities:** Mix dos dois estilos.

**5. Important City Places:**
- **Chemist's / Pharmacy:** No Reino Unido, a farmácia é chamada de *"chemist's"* — *"Go to the chemist's for medicine."*
- **Grocery store / Supermarket:** Nos EUA, *"grocery store"* é mais comum para supermercados menores.
- **Restroom / Toilet / Bathroom:** Nos EUA: *"restroom"* ou *"bathroom"*. No Reino Unido: *"toilet"* ou *"loo"*.
- **Sidewalk / Pavement:** Nos EUA: *"sidewalk"* (calçada). No Reino Unido: *"pavement"*.

**6. Common Signs You'll See:**
- **OPEN / CLOSED** — Aberto / Fechado
- **ENTRANCE / EXIT** — Entrada / Saída
- **NO PARKING** — Proibido estacionar
- **HOSPITAL — QUIET PLEASE** — Silêncio por favor
- **EMERGENCY** — Emergência
- **ONE WAY** — Mão única
