## Script

### Opening

**Emma:** Hello, everyone! Welcome back to English Conversations! I'm Emma.

**Lucas:** And I'm Lucas! Hi everyone! How are you today?

**Emma:** I'm great, thank you! Lucas, today we have two very important topics to talk about!

**Lucas:** Yes! We're going to talk about numbers and ages — and then about family!

**Emma:** Perfect! These are things we use every single day. Let's start!

---

### Main Conversation Part 1: Numbers & Age

**Emma:** Lucas, let me ask you something. How old are you?

**Lucas:** I am twenty-eight years old! And you, Emma? How old are you?

**Emma:** I am twenty-five years old! We are young!

**Lucas:** (laughs) Yes, we are! Numbers are very important in English, right?

**Emma:** They are! Let's practice together. Lucas, can you count from one to ten?

**Lucas:** Of course! One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine, ten!

**Emma:** Excellent! Now the teens — eleven to twenty. These can be tricky!

**Lucas:** Yes! Eleven, twelve, thirteen, fourteen, fifteen, sixteen, seventeen, eighteen, nineteen, twenty!

**Emma:** Very good! And remember — thirteen, fourteen, fifteen... they all end in T-E-E-N.

**Lucas:** That's right! And the tens: twenty, thirty, forty, fifty, sixty, seventy, eighty, ninety, one hundred!

**Emma:** Perfect! And how do we combine them? For example, twenty-one, thirty-five, forty-seven...

**Lucas:** Easy! Just say the ten, then the unit. Like: forty — seven. Forty-seven!

**Emma:** Now, let's practice asking about age. How do we ask someone's age in English?

**Lucas:** Simple! We say: "How old are you?" And the answer is: "I am ___ years old."

**Emma:** Let's practice with family! Lucas, how old is your mother?

**Lucas:** My mother is fifty-five years old. And my father is fifty-eight years old.

**Emma:** My mother is fifty years old. And my father is fifty-two. They are not old — they are experienced!

**Lucas:** (laughs) I love that! "Experienced!" 

---

### Main Conversation Part 2: Family & Relationships

**Emma:** OK, now let's talk about family! Lucas, do you have a big family or a small family?

**Lucas:** I have a medium family. I have one brother and my parents. What about you?

**Emma:** I have a bigger family! I have one brother and one sister. 

**Lucas:** Nice! And how do we talk about family in English? What are the important words?

**Emma:** Great question! Let's review. Father is "pai." Mother is "mãe." Brother is "irmão." Sister is "irmã."

**Lucas:** And parents — that means both father and mother together. "My parents are amazing!"

**Emma:** And we have more! Grandfather is "avô." Grandmother is "avó." Son is "filho." Daughter is "filha."

**Lucas:** And husband and wife for married couples! "He is her husband. She is his wife."

**Emma:** Now — very important — possessive adjectives! This is how we show something belongs to someone.

**Lucas:** Right! "MY family." "YOUR brother." "HIS father." "HER mother." "OUR parents." "THEIR children."

**Emma:** Let's use them in sentences! For example: "This is my father. His name is Carlos."

**Lucas:** I love that structure — "This is my..." Let me try! "This is my brother. His name is Thiago. He is thirty years old."

**Emma:** Perfect! And we also use "I have" to talk about family members.

**Lucas:** Yes! "I have one brother." "I have two sisters." "She has a big family."

**Emma:** And the negative: "I don't have brothers." "He doesn't have sisters."

**Lucas:** One more thing — plurals! Brother becomes brothers. Sister becomes sisters.

**Emma:** But be careful! Child becomes CHILDREN — not "childs!" That's irregular!

**Lucas:** Children! Very important word. "They have three children." Not "three childs."

**Emma:** (laughs) Exactly! Let's do a quick practice together. I'll say a sentence — you change it!

**Lucas:** Ready!

**Emma:** "I have one brother." Now say it with two brothers!

**Lucas:** "I have two brothers!" Easy!

**Emma:** "She has one child." Now with three children!

**Lucas:** "She has three children!" Got it!

---

### Closing & Practice

**Emma:** Wonderful! Now, dear listeners — it's YOUR turn!

**Lucas:** Yes! First, practice your numbers. Count from one to one hundred. Start slow, then go faster!

**Emma:** Then, practice asking ages. Ask someone in your family: "How old are you?" Write the answer in English!

**Lucas:** And talk about your family! Say: "This is my father. His name is ___. He is ___ years old."

**Emma:** Use possessive adjectives: MY, YOUR, HIS, HER, OUR, THEIR. These are very important!

**Lucas:** And remember — "I have" for yourself. "She has" and "He has" for other people!

**Emma:** You're doing great! English is fun when we practice every day!

**Lucas:** Thank you so much for listening, everyone!

**Emma:** See you next time!

**Both:** Bye-bye!

## Key Grammar & Vocabulary Used

### From Lesson 02 (Numbers & Age):
- ✅ Numbers 1–100 (one, two, three... twenty, thirty, forty...)
- ✅ Teen numbers: thirteen, fourteen, fifteen... (ending in -teen)
- ✅ Combined numbers: twenty-one, thirty-five, forty-seven
- ✅ "How old are you?" → "I am ___ years old."
- ✅ Verb "to be" with age: I am, he is, she is, they are
- ✅ Age vocabulary: old, young, years old

### From Lesson 03 (Family & Relationships):
- ✅ Core family: father, mother, brother, sister, parents, family
- ✅ Extended family: grandfather, grandmother, son, daughter, husband, wife
- ✅ Possessive adjectives: my, your, his, her, our, their
- ✅ Structure "This is my..." / "That is my..."
- ✅ "I have / She has / He has"
- ✅ Negative: "I don't have" / "He doesn't have"
- ✅ Plural forms: brothers, sisters, children (irregular)
