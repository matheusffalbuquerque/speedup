## Slide 3: Future with "Will"

### Predictions and General Future Ideas

Use **will** to talk about predictions, promises, and general ideas about the future.

```text
Subject + will + base verb
```

### Examples

- "I will speak English better in the future."
- "She will achieve her goals."
- "Technology will change many jobs."
- "We will need English for work and travel."
- "They will be successful with practice."

### Negative Form

```text
Subject + will not + base verb
will not = won't
```

- "I won't give up."
- "He won't stop studying."
- "They won't forget their goals."

---

## Slide 4: Future with "Going To"

### Plans and Intentions

Use **going to** when you already have a plan or intention.

```text
Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb
```

### Examples

- "I am going to study English tonight."
- "She is going to apply for a new job."
- "We are going to travel next year."
- "They are going to start a small business."
- "He is going to practice every day."

### Short Forms

| Full Form | Short Form |
|-----------|------------|
| I am going to | I'm going to |
| She is going to | She's going to |
| We are going to | We're going to |
| They are going to | They're going to |

---

## Slide 5: "Will" vs "Going To"

### Choosing the Right Future Form

| Use | Structure | Example |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| Prediction | **will** | "I think she will be successful." |
| Promise | **will** | "I will practice more." |
| General future idea | **will** | "English will help my career." |
| Plan | **going to** | "I'm going to study tonight." |
| Intention | **going to** | "He's going to change jobs." |
| Already decided action | **going to** | "We're going to travel in July." |

### Compare

- "I will learn English." = general future idea / promise
- "I am going to study English every Monday." = plan
- "She will have a great future." = prediction
- "She is going to take an English course." = intention

## Slide 6: Expressions for Goals and Dreams

### Natural Ways to Talk About the Future

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **I want to...** | desejo / objetivo | "I want to improve my English." |
| **I hope to...** | esperança | "I hope to travel next year." |
| **I plan to...** | plano | "I plan to study at university." |
| **I dream of...** | sonho | "I dream of living abroad." |
| **My goal is to...** | meta | "My goal is to get a promotion." |
| **My dream is to...** | sonho | "My dream is to open a restaurant." |

### Important Patterns

```text
I want to + base verb
I hope to + base verb
I plan to + base verb
My goal is to + base verb
My dream is to + base verb
I dream of + verb-ing / noun
```

### Examples

- "I want to speak English with confidence."
- "I hope to work for an international company."
- "I plan to save money this year."
- "I dream of studying in another country."

## Slide 7: Talking About Ambition and Success

### Building Stronger Sentences

You can make your future sentences stronger by adding a reason.

```text
Future goal + because + reason
```

### Model Sentences

- "I want to learn English because I need it for work."
- "She is going to study more because she wants a better job."
- "He dreams of opening a business because he likes helping people."
- "We will practice every week because we want to improve."
- "My goal is to travel because I want to learn about new cultures."

### Useful Reason Words

| Word | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **because** | reason | "I study because I want to improve." |
| **so** | result | "I practice every day, so I will improve." |
| **for** | purpose / benefit | "English is important for my career." |

## Slide 8: Reading Model — My Future Plans

### Short Text

> Lucas has many goals for the future. He wants to improve his English because he dreams of working for an international company. Next year, he is going to take a new course and practice speaking every week. He hopes to travel to Canada one day and meet people from different cultures. Lucas believes English will help his career, and he says, "I won't give up. My dream is important to me."

### Language Highlights

- **goals for the future**
- **wants to improve**
- **dreams of working**
- **is going to take**
- **hopes to travel**
- **will help his career**
- **I won't give up**

## Slide 9: Future Questions

### Asking About Goals and Dreams

Use these questions to talk about future plans and ambitions.

| Question | Model Answer |
|----------|--------------|
| **What is your dream?** | "My dream is to travel the world." |
| **What is your goal?** | "My goal is to speak English well." |
| **What are you going to do next year?** | "I'm going to study more." |
| **What do you want to achieve?** | "I want to achieve success in my career." |
| **Do you hope to live abroad?** | "Yes, I hope to live abroad one day." |
| **Will English help your future?** | "Yes, English will help my future." |

### Useful Answer Starters

- "My dream is to..."
- "My goal is to..."
- "I'm going to..."
- "I want to..."
- "I hope to..."
- "I think I will..."
