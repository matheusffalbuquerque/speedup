## Slide 3: Present Simple for Customs & Habits

### Talking About What People Always Do

Use the **Present Simple** to describe cultural habits, traditions, and things that happen regularly.

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **People + verb** | "People celebrate with music and food." |
| **Subject + always/usually + verb** | "Families usually gather at Christmas." |
| **Subject + do/does not + verb** | "Some people don't celebrate this holiday." |

### Model Sentences

- "Brazilians celebrate Carnival every year."
- "People wear costumes on Halloween."
- "Families eat special food on holidays."
- "The ceremony usually takes place at night."
- "Many cultures celebrate the new year with fireworks."

---

## Slide 4: Comparing Cultures

### "In [Country], we/they..."

Use this structure to compare how different countries celebrate or do things.

```text
In [country], we/they + verb...
```

### Examples

- "In Brazil, we celebrate Carnival with samba."
- "In the USA, they celebrate Thanksgiving in November."
- "In Japan, they have the Cherry Blossom Festival."
- "In Mexico, they celebrate Día de los Muertos."
- "In Australia, Christmas is in summer."

### Useful Contrast Connector

- "In Brazil, we eat rice and beans. **In Japan, they** eat rice and fish."
- "In the USA, they give presents at Christmas. **In Brazil, we** also give presents."

## Slide 5: Cultural Expressions

### Common Ways to Talk About Culture

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| **It's a tradition to...** | "It's a tradition to wear new clothes on New Year's." |
| **We always...** | "We always eat together on Christmas." |
| **People usually...** | "People usually watch fireworks on New Year's Eve." |
| **It's common to...** | "It's common to give flowers at weddings." |
| **It's important to...** | "It's important to respect other cultures." |

### More Examples

- "It's a tradition to light candles during Hanukkah."
- "We always watch the parade on national holidays."
- "It's common to send greeting cards on special occasions."
- "People usually wear white at New Year's in Brazil."

- "At Carnival, people **dance**, **sing**, and **wear costumes**."
- "At Thanksgiving, families **gather** and **eat** together."
- "At Diwali, people **light lamps** and **celebrate with sweets**."

## Slide 7: Reading Model — A Cultural Comparison

### Short Text

> Maria is from Brazil and her friend Anna is from the USA. They talk about how their countries celebrate the new year. Maria says: "In Brazil, we usually wear white and go to the beach. We eat lentils because it's a tradition for good luck." Anna says: "In the USA, people watch fireworks and celebrate with family. It's common to make new year resolutions." Both girls agree: every culture has special customs, and it's important to respect and learn about them.

### Language Highlights

- **In Brazil, we usually wear white**
- **it's a tradition for good luck**
- **In the USA, people watch fireworks**
- **It's common to make resolutions**
- **every culture has special customs**
- **it's important to respect**
