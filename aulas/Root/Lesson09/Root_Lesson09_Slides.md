## Slide 4: Sports Verbs — Play, Do, Go

### How English Organizes Sports Actions

| Verb | Use | Examples |
|------|-----|----------|
| **play** | team sports and ball sports | **play soccer**, **play basketball**, **play volleyball** |
| **do** | exercise and individual activities | **do exercise**, **do yoga**, **do karate** |
| **go** | movement activities in **-ing** form | **go swimming**, **go running**, **go cycling** |

### Quick Rule

- Use **play** with games and sports
- Use **do** with routines and exercises
- Use **go** with **-ing** activities

## Slide 5: "I like playing..." vs. "I like to play..."

### Two Correct Structures

| Structure | Main Idea | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **like + verb-ing** | general preference | "I like playing basketball." |
| **like + to + base verb** | preference connected to an action or routine | "I like to play basketball after class." |

### Meaning in Context

- "I like **playing** soccer." → focus on the activity in general
- "I like **to play** soccer on weekends." → focus on the action in a routine or situation

### More Examples

- "She likes swimming."
- "He likes to run before work."
- "We like playing as a team."

## Slide 6: Frequency Adverbs

### Talking About Habit and Frequency

| Adverb | Português | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Always** | sempre | "She always stretches before exercise." |
| **Often** | frequentemente | "He often plays soccer after work." |
| **Frequently** | com frequência | "They frequently go running together." |
| **Sometimes** | às vezes | "I sometimes go to the gym at night." |
| **Rarely** | raramente | "We rarely play basketball during the week." |
| **Hardly ever** | quase nunca | "She hardly ever swims in winter." |
| **Never** | nunca | "He never misses team practice." |

### Position in the Sentence

- Before the main verb: "I **often play** soccer."
- After the verb **be**: "She **is always** active."

## Slide 7: Talking About Sports Routines

### Sentence Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Subject + play + sport** | "They play basketball on Fridays." |
| **Subject + do + activity** | "I do exercise at home." |
| **Subject + go + activity-ing** | "She goes swimming every weekend." |
| **Subject + frequency adverb + verb** | "He often runs in the evening." |
| **Subject + likes + activity** | "My brother likes playing soccer." |

### Natural Examples

- "I often go to the gym before work."
- "She rarely plays team sports."
- "They frequently practice in the afternoon."
- "He likes to swim, but he hardly ever goes to the pool."

---

## Slide 8: Reading Model — Active Routine

### Short Text

> Lucas likes sports and outdoor activities. He often plays soccer with friends on Saturdays, and he frequently goes running in the morning. During the week, he goes to the gym after work and does exercise for about one hour. He also likes swimming, but he hardly ever goes to the pool because it is far from his house. Lucas says that regular practice helps him stay active and healthy.

### Language Highlights

- **likes sports**
- **often plays soccer**
- **frequently goes running**
- **goes to the gym**
- **does exercise**
- **hardly ever goes to the pool**

| "They often goes running." | "They often go running." |

### Correct Model Sentences

- "I like playing soccer."
