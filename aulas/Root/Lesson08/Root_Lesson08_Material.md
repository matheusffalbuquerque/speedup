## 📚 Grammar — Structure 1: "What do you do?" / "I work as a..."

### Asking about jobs

Use these questions to ask about a person's profession:

- **What do you do?**
- **What do you do for work?**
- **Where do you work?**
- **What does she do?**

> In everyday English, **What do you do?** usually means **What is your job?**

---

### Answering naturally

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **I work as a/an + profession** | "I work as a teacher." |
| **I am a/an + profession** | "I am an engineer." |
| **I work in + place/area** | "I work in an office." |
| **I work for + company** | "I work for a small company." |

### More examples

- "I work as a receptionist in a clinic."
- "She is a designer."
- "They work in sales."
- "My father works for a transport company."

---

### Articles with professions

Use **a/an** before singular professions:

- "I am **a** teacher."
- "He is **an** engineer."
- "She is **a** doctor."

**Common mistake:**

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| "I am teacher." | "I am a teacher." |
| "She is engineer." | "She is an engineer." |

## 📚 Grammar — Structure 2: Present Perfect with "for" and "since"

### When do we use it?

We use the **Present Perfect** with **for** and **since** to talk about actions or situations that started in the past and continue now.

- "I **have worked** here **for** three years."
- "She **has lived** in this city **since** 2022."

This structure is very common when talking about jobs, careers, and routines.

---

### Form

**Subject + have/has + past participle + for/since + time expression**

| Subject | Auxiliary | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | **have** | "We **have worked** here for two years." |
| He / She / It | **has** | "She **has worked** here since January." |

---

### "For" vs. "Since"

| Word | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **for** | duration / period of time | "I have worked here **for** five months." |
| **since** | starting point | "I have worked here **since** May." |

### Examples

- "He has had this job **for** a long time."
- "I have studied English **for** two years."
- "She has worked in that office **since** 2024."
- "They have known their boss **since** last year."

---

### ✅ Affirmative

- "I **have worked** here for six months."
- "She **has been** a teacher since 2021."
- "We **have had** the same boss for three years."

### ❌ Negative

- "I **haven't worked** in an office before."
- "He **hasn't had** an interview since last year."
- "They **haven't been** employees here for very long."

### ❓ Questions

- "**Have** you **worked** here for a long time?"
- "**Has** she **worked** in this profession since 2023?"
- "**How long have** you worked here?"

### Short answers

- **Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.**
- **Yes, she has. / No, she hasn't.**

---

### Time expressions often used with jobs

| Expression | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **for two years** | "I have worked here for two years." |
| **since January** | "She has worked here since January." |
| **since 2020** | "He has lived here since 2020." |
| **for a long time** | "They have known each other for a long time." |
| **all my life** | "My mother has worked in education all her life." |

## 📚 Grammar — Structure 3: Job-Related Questions and Answers

### Useful questions

- "What do you do?"
- "Where do you work?"
- "How long have you worked there?"
- "Do you like your job?"
- "What time do you start work?"
- "Do you work alone or with a team?"
- "Have you ever had a job interview in English?"

### Useful answers

- "I work as a nurse."
- "I work in a hospital."
- "I have worked there for four years."
- "Yes, I do. My job is challenging but rewarding."
- "I start work at 8 a.m."
- "I usually work with a small team."
- "No, I haven't, but I would like to prepare for one."

## 🎧 Listening Practice

### Teacher Reading 1

> "My name is Bruno, and I work as a receptionist in a hotel. I have worked there for two years. My shift starts at 7 a.m., and I usually talk to guests, answer phone calls, and help with reservations. My boss is demanding, but my coworkers are friendly."

**Questions:**
1. What does Bruno do?
2. Where does he work?
3. How long has he worked there?
4. What time does his shift start?
5. How does he describe his boss and coworkers?

---

### Teacher Reading 2

> "Camila is an engineer. She works for a technology company, and she has worked there since 2023. She likes her profession because she learns new things every week. Right now, she is preparing for an important interview for a manager position with a higher salary."

**Questions:**
1. What is Camila's profession?
2. What type of company does she work for?
3. Since when has she worked there?
4. Why does she like her profession?
5. What is she preparing for?

## 🗣️ Speaking Practice

### A. Personal Work Profile

Answer the questions with your teacher or a partner:

1. What do you do?
2. Where do you work or study?
3. Do you like your routine?
4. How long have you studied or worked there?
5. What is difficult about your routine?
6. What profession would you like to try in the future?

### B. Job Interview Mini Practice

Use short answers to practice:

- "Tell me about your experience."
- "Why do you want this job?"
- "What are your strengths?"
- "Have you worked with customers before?"
- "How long have you studied English?"

### C. Compare Two Professions

Use comparative ideas when possible:

- teacher vs. doctor
- office job vs. remote job
- day shift vs. night shift

Try to use:
- **salary**
- **schedule**
- **boss**
- **experience**

## 📖 Reading Practice

### A New Job Opportunity

Rafaela works as a salesperson in a department store. She has worked there for three years, and she knows the job very well. Her schedule is busy, and she often works on weekends, but she likes talking to customers and helping them choose products.

Recently, Rafaela saw a new job opportunity at a marketing company. The position is for an office assistant, and the salary is better than her current one. She has updated her resume and prepared for the interview. She feels nervous because she has never had a job interview in English before, but she is excited too.

Her best friend says she has enough experience and good communication skills. Rafaela agrees, but she also knows she needs to practice answering questions about her career, her strengths, and her goals.

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What does Rafaela do now?
2. How long has she worked there?
3. Why is the new job attractive?
4. What has Rafaela done to prepare?
5. Why does she feel nervous?
6. What does she need to practice?
