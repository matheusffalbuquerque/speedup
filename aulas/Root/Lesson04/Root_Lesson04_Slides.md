# Root - Lesson 04: Food & Restaurants
**Nível:** A1 Avançado / A2 Iniciante  
**Duração:** 60 minutos  
**Tema:** Comida e restaurantes

---

## 🎯 Objetivos da Aula
- Aprender vocabulário essencial de restaurantes (menu, order, waiter, bill, tip, etc.)
- Usar o modal "would" para fazer pedidos educados
- Praticar estruturas de conversa em restaurante ("I'd like to...", "Can I have...?")
- Simular situações reais: reservar mesa, pedir prato, pagar a conta

---

## 🎲 Warm-up (5-10 min)
**Menu Speed Reading**

**Instruções:**
1. Professor escreve ou projeta um cardápio simples (3-4 categorias: Appetizers, Main Dishes, Desserts, Drinks)
2. Dê 60 segundos para os alunos lerem o menu em silêncio
3. Esconda o menu e faça perguntas rápidas:
   - "What are the appetizers on the menu?"
   - "How much is the pasta dish?"
   - "What desserts are available?"
4. O aluno que responder corretamente primeiro marca um ponto
5. Com 5 alunos: jogue em equipes de 2+2+1, quem acertar mais perguntas vence

**Menu sugerido para o warm-up:**
- **Appetizers:** Soup of the day ($5) | Garlic bread ($4) | Salad ($6)
- **Main Dishes:** Grilled chicken ($14) | Pasta Carbonara ($12) | Fish and chips ($15)
- **Desserts:** Chocolate cake ($6) | Ice cream ($4) | Fruit salad ($5)
- **Drinks:** Water ($2) | Juice ($3) | Coffee ($3)

**Objetivo:** Ativar vocabulário de comida e restaurante, criar contexto para a aula

## 📚 Presentation (20 min)

### Slide 1: Restaurant Vocabulary
**Essential Restaurant Words:**
- **Menu** = Cardápio
- **Order** = Pedido / Pedir
- **Waiter / Waitress** = Garçom / Garçonete
- **Reservation** = Reserva
- **Dish** = Prato (refeição)
- **Appetizer** = Entrada / Aperitivo
- **Dessert** = Sobremesa
- **Bill / Check** = Conta
- **Tip** = Gorjeta

**Related Words:**
- **Table** = Mesa
- **Seat** = Assento / Lugar
- **Napkin** = Guardanapo
- **Fork / Knife / Spoon** = Garfo / Faca / Colher
- **Chef / Cook** = Chef / Cozinheiro

**Example:** "The waiter brought the menu. I looked at the dishes and decided to order the pasta. At the end, I paid the bill and left a tip."

---

### Slide 2: Modal "Would" for Polite Requests
**Structure:**
- Subject + **would** + base verb

**Forms:**
- ✅ **Affirmative:** I/You/He/She/We/They **would** like...
- ❌ **Negative:** I **wouldn't** like...
- ❓ **Question:** **Would** you like...?

**Contraction:** would = 'd → "I would like" = "I'd like"

**Examples:**
- "**I'd like** a table for two, please."
- "**She'd like** the chicken dish."
- "**Would** you **like** to see the dessert menu?"
- "**I wouldn't like** anything else, thank you."

**Key Point:** "Would" makes requests sound polite and respectful — always use it in restaurants!

---

### Slide 3: Ordering Food — "I'd like to..." / "Can I have...?"
**Two Main Structures:**

**"I'd like to..." / "I'd like + noun"**
- Form: I'd like + noun / I'd like to + verb
- Use: Polite, slightly more formal
- "**I'd like** the grilled chicken, please."
- "**I'd like to order** the pasta."
- "**I'd like** a glass of water."

**"Can I have...?"**
- Form: Can I have + noun?
- Use: Direct, common in everyday speech
- "**Can I have** the menu, please?"
- "**Can I have** a table for three?"
- "**Can I have** the bill?"

**Comparison:**
| | More Formal | Everyday |
|---|-------------|----------|
| Order food | I'd like the steak. | Can I have the steak? |
| Ask for water | I'd like some water. | Can I have some water? |
| Request bill | I'd like the bill. | Can I have the bill? |

---

### Slide 4: Restaurant Roles — Waiter vs Customer
**The Waiter says:**
- "Good evening! Do you have a reservation?"
- "Right this way, please."
- "Are you ready to order?"
- "What would you like to start with?"
- "Would you like any dessert?"
- "Can I get you anything else?"
- "Here is your bill."
- "Enjoy your meal!"

**The Customer says:**
- "I have a reservation for [name]."
- "A table for two, please."
- "Can I see the menu?"
- "I'd like to order the pasta."
- "Can I have the bill, please?"
- "This dish is delicious!"
- "Could we have a little more time?"
- "Thank you, it was wonderful!"

---

### Slide 5: Making a Reservation
**Phone Conversation Structure:**

**Waiter:** "Good afternoon, [Restaurant Name]. How can I help you?"
**Customer:** "I'd like to make a reservation, please."
**Waiter:** "Of course! For how many people?"
**Customer:** "For three people."
**Waiter:** "What day and time would you like?"
**Customer:** "This Saturday at 7:30 PM."
**Waiter:** "And your name, please?"
**Customer:** "It's [Name]."
**Waiter:** "Perfect! We'll see you Saturday at 7:30. Have a great day!"
**Customer:** "Thank you! Goodbye!"

**Key Phrases:**
- "I'd like to make a reservation." = Gostaria de fazer uma reserva.
- "For [number] people." = Para [número] pessoas.
- "At [time] on [day]." = Às [horário] de [dia].
- "Under the name [Name]." = No nome [Nome].

---

### Slide 6: Paying the Bill
**End of Meal Expressions:**

**Asking for the Bill:**
- "Can I have the bill, please?"
- "Could we have the check?"
- "We're ready to pay."

**When the Bill Arrives:**
- "Is the tip included?" = A gorjeta está incluída?
- "We'll split the bill." = Vamos dividir a conta.
- "I'll pay for everyone." = Eu pago por todos.
- "Keep the change." = Pode ficar com o troco.

**Complimenting the Meal:**
- "Everything was delicious!" = Tudo estava delicioso!
- "The food was excellent!" = A comida estava excelente!
- "We really enjoyed it, thank you!" = Adoramos, obrigado!

**Tip Culture (English-speaking countries):**
- Standard tip = 15–20% of the bill
- "The service was great, so I left a good tip."

## 💬 Practice (20 min)

### Activity 1: What Do You Order? (10 min)

**Instructions:**
1. Each student receives (or draws) a simple restaurant menu
2. Ask classmates: "What would you like to order?"
3. Students respond with their choices using "I'd like..." or "Can I have...?"
4. Follow-up questions:
   - "Why did you choose that dish?"
   - "What appetizer would you like?"
   - "Would you like dessert?"
5. Students report to the class: "Maria would like the grilled chicken. João would like the pasta."

**Possible answers:**
- "I'd like the soup as an appetizer and the fish as a main dish."
- "Can I have the chocolate cake for dessert?"
- "I'd like a glass of juice, please."

---

### Activity 2: Restaurant Role-play (10 min)

**Scenario:** A group of friends is having dinner at a restaurant.

**Student A (Waiter):**
- Greet customers and ask about reservation
- Give menus and take orders
- Describe specials of the day
- Bring the bill at the end

**Student B / C / D (Customers):**
- Make a reservation or ask for a table
- Study the menu and ask questions
- Order appetizers, main dishes, and desserts
- Ask for the bill and decide about the tip

**Phrases to use:**
- "Good evening! Do you have a reservation?"
- "I'd like to order the..."
- "Can I have the...?"
- "What's the dish of the day?"
- "Could we have the bill, please?"
- "Is the tip included?"

**Switch roles after 5 minutes**

> **With 5 students:** 1 waiter + 2 customers (first round) → switch so all students play the waiter

## 📌 Teacher's Notes
- **Warm-up prep:** Prepare o menu do warm-up com antecedência — pode ser escrito no quadro ou impresso. Use preços simples para facilitar a memorização.
- **Vocabulário visual:** Se possível, mostre imagens de um menu real em inglês para contextualizar os termos "appetizer", "main course" e "dessert".
- **"Would" vs "Can":** Enfatize que ambas as estruturas são corretas, mas "would" é mais formal e educada — ideal para restaurantes e situações profissionais.
- **Erros comuns:** Alunos frequentemente dizem "I want the pasta" em vez de "I'd like the pasta" — corrija gentilmente explicando que em inglês, "want" direto pode soar rude em contextos formais.
- **Role-play:** Circule entre os grupos durante o role-play para corrigir pronúncia e oferecer vocabulário adicional quando necessário.
- **Gorjeta (Tip):** Explique brevemente a cultura de gorjeta nos países de língua inglesa (EUA: 15–20%), pois é um tema cultural relevante e interessante para os alunos.
- **Adaptação para turmas menores:** Com 1-2 alunos, professor pode assumir o papel de waiter ou customer e conduzir a conversação diretamente.
- **Extensão:** Para alunos mais avançados, peça que improvvisem reclamações educadas ("I'm sorry, but this dish is cold. Could I have another one?") para ampliar o vocabulário.
