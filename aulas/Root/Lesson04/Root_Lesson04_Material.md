## 📚 Grammar Review

### 1. Modal "Would" for Polite Requests

**Form:** Subject + **would** + base verb  
**Contraction:** would → **'d** (I would = **I'd**)

**Use:** To make requests sound polite and respectful — very common in restaurants, hotels, and formal situations.

**Affirmative:**
- **I'd like** a table for two, please.
- **She'd like** the pasta dish.
- **We'd like** to see the dessert menu.
- **They'd like** a reservation for Saturday.

**Negative:**
- **I wouldn't like** anything else, thank you.
- **He wouldn't like** the spicy dish — he doesn't eat spicy food.

**Interrogative (Questions):**
- **Would you like** to order now?
- **Would you like** to see the menu?
- **What would you like** for dessert?
- **Would you like** still or sparkling water?

**Short Answers:**
- Yes, I would. / No, I wouldn't.
- Yes, please. / No, thank you.

**⚠️ Common Mistakes:**

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct |
|-------------|------------|
| I would like order the pasta. | I **would like to order** the pasta. |
| Would you like eat dessert? | Would you like **to eat** dessert? |
| I want the chicken. *(rude in formal context)* | **I'd like** the chicken, please. |
| Would you liked a coffee? | **Would you like** a coffee? |

---

### 2. "I'd like to..." vs "Can I have...?"

**Both are correct and common in restaurants. Here's when to use each:**

| | "I'd like to..." | "Can I have...?" |
|---|------------------|------------------|
| **Formality** | More formal / polite | Everyday / casual |
| **Best for** | Restaurants, hotels, formal situations | Cafes, casual places, quick orders |
| **Structure** | I'd like + noun / I'd like to + verb | Can I have + noun? |

**"I'd like..." Examples:**
- I'd like **the grilled chicken**, please.
- I'd like **to order** the pasta Carbonara.
- I'd like **a glass of still water**.
- I'd like **the bill**, please.

**"Can I have...?" Examples:**
- Can I have **the menu**, please?
- Can I have **a table for three**?
- Can I have **the chocolate cake** for dessert?
- Can I have **the check**, please?

**💡 Tip:** In English-speaking restaurants, both expressions are polite. Always add "please" to sound even more courteous!

## 💡 Useful Expressions

### Making a Reservation
- **I'd like to make a reservation.** = Gostaria de fazer uma reserva.
- **A table for [number], please.** = Uma mesa para [número], por favor.
- **For this Saturday at 7 PM.** = Para este sábado às 19h.
- **Under the name [Name].** = No nome [Nome].
- **Do you have any tables available?** = Vocês têm mesas disponíveis?

### Arriving at the Restaurant
- **I have a reservation under [Name].** = Tenho uma reserva no nome [Nome].
- **We don't have a reservation.** = Não temos reserva.
- **Can we sit by the window?** = Podemos sentar perto da janela?
- **Can I see the menu, please?** = Posso ver o cardápio, por favor?

### Ordering
- **I'd like to order the...** = Gostaria de pedir o/a...
- **What do you recommend?** = O que você recomenda?
- **What's the dish of the day?** = Qual é o prato do dia?
- **Is this dish spicy?** = Este prato é apimentado?
- **Does this contain [ingredient]?** = Este prato contém [ingrediente]?
- **I'll have the same.** = Vou querer o mesmo.
- **Could we have a little more time?** = Podemos ter mais um tempinho?

### During the Meal
- **Enjoy your meal!** = Bom apetite! (dito pelo garçom)
- **Thank you, it looks delicious!** = Obrigado, parece delicioso!
- **Could I have some more water, please?** = Poderia me trazer mais água, por favor?
- **Excuse me!** = Com licença! (para chamar o garçom)

### Paying the Bill
- **Can I have the bill, please?** = Pode trazer a conta, por favor?
- **Is the tip included?** = A gorjeta está incluída?
- **We'll split the bill.** = Vamos dividir a conta.
- **Keep the change.** = Pode ficar com o troco.
- **Everything was delicious, thank you!** = Tudo estava delicioso, obrigado!

## 🎧 Listening Practice

### At the Restaurant

*Listen to the conversation and answer the questions below.*

---

**Characters:** Waiter (W), Customer 1 — Ana (A), Customer 2 — Tom (T)

---

**W:** Good evening! Welcome to La Bella. Do you have a reservation?

**A:** Yes, we do. I made a reservation for two. It's under Ana Silva.

**W:** Perfect, Ms. Silva! Right this way, please. Here's your table. Can I get you something to drink while you look at the menu?

**T:** Yes, please. I'd like a glass of sparkling water.

**A:** And I'd like a glass of orange juice, please.

**W:** Of course! Here are your menus. I'll be right back with your drinks.

*(A few minutes later)*

**W:** Here are your drinks. Are you ready to order?

**A:** Almost! What do you recommend for the main course?

**W:** Tonight's special is the grilled salmon with vegetables — it's very popular. We also have an excellent pasta carbonara.

**T:** That sounds great! I'd like to order the grilled salmon, please.

**A:** And I'd like the pasta carbonara. Oh, and could we have some garlic bread as an appetizer?

**W:** Absolutely! So that's one grilled salmon, one pasta carbonara, and garlic bread to start. Is that correct?

**T:** Yes, that's right.

**W:** Perfect! I'll have that right out for you.

*(After the main course)*

**W:** How was everything? Did you enjoy your meal?

**A:** It was delicious! The pasta was amazing.

**T:** The salmon was excellent too. Thank you!

**W:** I'm so glad! Would you like to see the dessert menu?

**A:** Oh, why not? Can I have the chocolate cake, please?

**T:** And I'd like the fruit salad.

**W:** Great choices! I'll bring those right away.

*(After dessert)*

**T:** Excuse me! Could we have the bill, please?

**W:** Of course! Here you go. Is the tip included? No, it's not — feel free to add whatever you'd like.

**A:** The service was wonderful. Let's leave a good tip!

**T:** Agreed. Here you are. Keep the change, please.

**W:** Thank you so much! It was a pleasure having you. Have a lovely evening!

**A & T:** Thank you! Goodbye!

---

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. How did Ana and Tom get their table? (Did they have a reservation?)
2. What did Ana order to drink?
3. What was the special dish of the evening?
4. What did they order as an appetizer?
5. What did Ana choose for dessert?

## 🗣️ Speaking Practice

### Restaurant Role-play Cards

---

**Scenario 1: Making a Reservation (Phone Call)**

**Student A — The Caller (Customer):**
- You want to make a reservation for **4 people**
- You want a table for **next Friday at 8 PM**
- Your name is [your name]
- Ask if they have a window table available

**Student B — The Restaurant Staff:**
- Answer the phone: "Good afternoon, [Restaurant Name]. How can I help you?"
- Ask for: number of people, date, time, name
- Confirm the reservation
- Mention any special information (e.g., "We have a beautiful terrace!")

**Useful phrases:**
- "I'd like to make a reservation, please."
- "For how many people?"
- "What day and time would you like?"
- "Your reservation is confirmed!"

---

**Scenario 2: Ordering Food at the Restaurant**

**Student A — The Waiter:**
- Welcome the customers
- Offer the menu
- Describe the special of the day: "Grilled chicken with rice and salad — $13"
- Take orders for appetizer, main course, drinks, and dessert
- Check on the customers during the meal

**Student B / C — The Customers:**
- Ask about the special of the day
- One of you is vegetarian — ask if there are vegetarian options
- Order different dishes for each course
- Ask for recommendations

**Useful phrases:**
- "What would you recommend?"
- "Is there a vegetarian option?"
- "I'd like the... and could I have... on the side?"
- "That sounds delicious! I'll have that."

---

**Scenario 3: Dealing with a Problem & Paying the Bill**

**Student A — The Customer:**
- Your dish arrived cold — politely tell the waiter
- Ask for the bill at the end
- Decide whether to split the bill or one person pays
- Leave a tip if the service was good

**Student B — The Waiter:**
- Apologize for the cold dish and offer to replace it
- Bring the new dish quickly
- Present the bill at the end
- Thank customers for their visit

**Useful phrases:**
- "Excuse me, I think my dish is cold. Could I have another one, please?"
- "I'm so sorry about that! I'll bring a new one right away."
- "Can I have the bill, please? We'll split it."
- "Is the tip included?"

> **With 4-5 students:** One student plays the waiter, others play customers. Rotate roles so everyone practices being the waiter.

## 📖 Reading Practice

### A Perfect Dinner

Last Saturday, my friend Lucas and I decided to go to a new Italian restaurant downtown. I called ahead and made a reservation for two at 7:30 PM. When we arrived, the waiter showed us to our table by the window — it was a lovely spot. We looked at the menu together and the waiter recommended the pasta carbonara as the dish of the day. Lucas ordered the carbonara, and I'd like the grilled chicken as my main course. We also asked for garlic bread as an appetizer, and it was amazing! For dessert, we both chose the chocolate cake. At the end of the evening, we asked for the bill. The total was $45, and we left a $9 tip because the service was excellent.

**Questions:**
1. Where did they go for dinner?
2. How did they get their table? Did they have a reservation?
3. What did the waiter recommend?
4. What did the narrator order for the main course?
5. How much was the tip they left?

## ✍️ Writing Practice

### Write About a Restaurant Experience

**Option A: Tell Your Story**

Think about a real or imaginary restaurant experience. Write **8–10 sentences** answering these questions:

- Where did you go? (restaurant name or type)
- Did you make a reservation?
- Who was the waiter / waitress?
- What did you order? (appetizer, main dish, dessert, drinks)
- How was the food? (use adjectives: delicious, amazing, excellent, terrible, etc.)
- Did you pay the bill? Did you leave a tip?
- Would you recommend this restaurant?

**Sentence starters to help you:**
- "Last [day/month], I went to..."
- "I made a reservation for..."
- "The waiter recommended..."
- "I'd like to order..."
- "For dessert, I chose..."
- "The bill was... and I left a tip of..."
- "I would definitely recommend this restaurant because..."

---

**Option B: Write a Restaurant Review**

Write a **short review** (6–8 sentences) for a restaurant. Imagine you are posting it online:

- Name of the restaurant
- Type of food (Italian, Brazilian, Japanese, etc.)
- What you ordered
- Quality of the food and service
- Star rating (⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐)
- Would you recommend it?

**Example opening:**  
*"I visited [Restaurant Name] last weekend and I highly recommend it! I made a reservation, and the waiter was very helpful..."*

|-----------|-----------|
| Order food | I'd like the chicken, please. |
| Ask for menu | Can I have the menu? |
| Ask for recommendation | What do you recommend? |
| Need more time | Could we have a little more time? |

### Modal "Would" Summary
| Form | Example |
|------|---------|
| ✅ Affirmative | I'd like a table for two. |
| ❌ Negative | I wouldn't like anything else. |
| ❓ Question | Would you like dessert? |
| Short answer | Yes, I would. / No, thank you. |

### Meal Sequence
1. **Reservation** → Call to book a table
2. **Arrival** → Host shows you to your table
3. **Menu** → Waiter brings the menu
4. **Appetizer** → First course (soup, salad, bread)
5. **Main course / Dish** → Principal meal
6. **Dessert** → Sweet final course
7. **Bill / Tip** → Pay and leave gratuity
