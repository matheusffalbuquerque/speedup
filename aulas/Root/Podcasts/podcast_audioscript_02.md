## Script

### Opening

**Alex:** Hi everyone! Welcome back to English Conversations. I'm Alex.

**Sam:** And I'm Sam! Great to be here. How are you, Alex?

**Alex:** Honestly? A little tired. I had a very busy weekend!

**Sam:** Oh really? Me too! I think that's a perfect topic for today.

**Alex:** Exactly! Today we're going to talk about past experiences — what we did recently — and then we're going to make some plans together.

**Sam:** Past and future — all in one episode. Let's go!

---

### Main Conversation Part 1: Last Weekend

**Alex:** So, Sam — what did you do last weekend?

**Sam:** Last Saturday, I went to a get-together at my friend's place. It was really fun!

**Alex:** Nice! How many people were there?

**Sam:** There were about ten people. We had a barbecue — a BBQ — in the backyard. I ate so much food!

**Alex:** (laughs) A BBQ sounds amazing. What did you eat?

**Sam:** We had grilled chicken, salad, and bread. And my friend made a delicious cake. I ate two slices!

**Alex:** Two slices! Did you cook anything?

**Sam:** Yes! I made a pasta salad. Everyone liked it. They said it was great!

**Alex:** Impressive! And what did you do after dinner?

**Sam:** We watched a movie together. It was a comedy — we laughed a lot. I stayed until midnight. I was really tired the next day!

**Alex:** I bet! What about Sunday? Did you rest?

**Sam:** Not really. On Sunday, I met my sister for lunch. We went to a new restaurant downtown. I had a burger — it was incredible. What about you, Alex? How was your weekend?

**Alex:** Well, I didn't go out much. On Friday night, I watched a concert on TV. The band was fantastic!

**Sam:** Oh, which band?

**Alex:** A rock band. I didn't know them before, but now I love their music! On Saturday, I worked in the morning. I didn't finish until 2 PM.

**Sam:** Oh no! You worked on a Saturday?

**Alex:** Unfortunately, yes. But in the afternoon, I went to the park. I walked for an hour and read a book. It was relaxing.

**Sam:** That sounds nice. Did you do anything on Sunday?

**Alex:** Sunday was quiet. I stayed home, cooked dinner, and went to bed early. I was exhausted!

---

### Main Conversation Part 2: Making Plans

**Sam:** Well, you deserve a fun weekend! Alex, my friends are having a party next Saturday. Would you like to come?

**Alex:** A party! What kind of party?

**Sam:** It's a birthday party — my friend Lucas is going to turn 30. There's going to be music, food, and dancing!

**Alex:** That sounds great! I'd love to come! What time does it start?

**Sam:** It starts at 7 PM. It's going to be at his apartment.

**Alex:** Perfect. Should I bring anything?

**Sam:** Just bring yourself! But maybe a birthday card would be nice.

**Alex:** Good idea. I'm going to buy a card tomorrow. So, what are you going to wear?

**Sam:** I don't know yet! Maybe something casual. It's not a formal event. How about you?

**Alex:** I'm going to wear jeans and a nice shirt. Simple but good!

**Sam:** Great! Oh, and — why don't we go together? We can meet at the subway station.

**Alex:** Count me in! That's a great idea. Let's meet at 6:30 PM.

**Sam:** Perfect! I'm going to text you the address later.

**Alex:** And what about after the party? Do you want to grab coffee somewhere?

**Sam:** Maybe! Let me check — I think I'm going to visit my parents on Sunday morning, so I can't stay out too late.

**Alex:** No problem. I'll let you know if I find a good place near Lucas's apartment.

**Sam:** Sounds like a plan!

---

### Closing

**Alex:** Now, dear listeners — your turn to practice!

**Sam:** First, think about last weekend. What did you do? Use past verbs: went, ate, saw, met, watched, worked...

**Alex:** Say it out loud: "Last Saturday, I went to..." or "Last night, I watched..."

**Sam:** Then, practice making an invitation! Say to someone: "Do you want to...?" or "Would you like to...?"

**Alex:** And practice accepting and declining too! "I'd love to!" or "I'm sorry, I can't."

**Sam:** Remember — for future plans, use "going to": "I'm going to go to a party!" "She's going to bring food!"

**Alex:** You're doing an amazing job. Keep practicing every day!

**Sam:** Thank you so much for listening!

**Alex:** See you next time!

**Both:** Bye-bye!

---

## Key Grammar & Vocabulary Used

### From Lesson 02 (Past Experiences):
- ✅ Simple Past irregular verbs: went, had, ate, made, met, said, saw, stayed, went, bought, worked
- ✅ Simple Past regular verbs: watched, walked, cooked, liked, finished, laughed
- ✅ Was/Were: "It was great!" / "We were tired" / "There were ten people"
- ✅ Negative: "I didn't go out" / "I didn't finish" / "I didn't know them"
- ✅ Questions with Did: "Did you cook?" / "Did you do anything?"
- ✅ Time expressions: last Saturday, last weekend, Friday night, on Sunday, the next day, until midnight

### From Lesson 03 (Making Plans & Invitations):
- ✅ Events vocabulary: party, barbecue/BBQ, get-together, concert
- ✅ Making invitations: "Would you like to come?" / "Why don't we go together?" / "Do you want to grab coffee?"
- ✅ Accepting: "I'd love to!" / "Count me in!" / "That sounds great!"
- ✅ Uncertain: "Let me check" / "I'll let you know"
- ✅ Time expressions: next Saturday, tonight, at 7 PM
- ✅ Future with "going to": "I'm going to buy a card" / "It's going to be at his apartment" / "She's going to bring food"
- ✅ Party vocabulary: birthday party, music, dancing, formal, casual
